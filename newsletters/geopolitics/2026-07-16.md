The global geopolitical landscape over the past 24-48 hours has been marked by escalating tensions in the **Middle East**, particularly concerning the **Strait of Hormuz**, renewed focus on **European** security dynamics amidst the ongoing conflict in **Ukraine**, and significant diplomatic shifts in the **Sahel** region of **Africa**. The confrontation between the **United States** and **Iran** has intensified with military strikes and counter-strikes, impacting global energy markets. Meanwhile, **European** nations are pursuing independent defense initiatives, and the **EU** continues its enlargement process. In **Africa**, a notable diplomatic reconciliation between **Algeria** and **Mali** signals shifting regional alignments.

## Middle East Tensions

The **Middle East** remains a focal point of international concern, with a significant escalation in the confrontation between the **United States** and **Iran** over the critical **Strait of Hormuz**. On July 15, 2026, **US Central Command** announced further strikes against **Iran** for approximately 90 minutes, following overnight attacks that **Tehran** claimed killed at least seven military personnel in southeastern **Iran**. These **US** strikes targeted **Iran's** coastal defense systems and cruise missile storage and launch sites. The **US** also reimposed a naval blockade on **Iranian** ports. **US President Donald Trump** reiterated threats to target **Iranian** civilian infrastructure if negotiations do not resume, and indicated more strikes were imminent in the coming days, potentially targeting bridges and power plants by next week.

In response, **Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps** (IRGC) stated it had launched strikes against **US** military targets in the region, including in **Bahrain**, **Kuwait**, and **Jordan**. **Jordan's** military reported intercepting three incoming **Iranian** missiles, while **Kuwait's** army stated its air defenses were repelling hostile drone assaults. **Bahrain's** defense force also confirmed intercepting **Iranian** attacks. The **IRGC** further threatened to close "all other export corridors that benefit the **U.S.** and its allies," emphasizing that regional energy exports would either be shared by all or denied to all. This follows **Iran's** assertion on July 13 that it had closed the **Strait of Hormuz**, a claim rejected by **President Trump** who declared the strait "open to ALL Ship traffic except for **Iran**."

The renewed **US** naval blockade has already had an impact on maritime traffic. Two tankers carrying **Iranian** oil, the **Rani** and the **Amil**, reportedly turned around after signaling **Karachi**, **Pakistan**, as their destination, as the blockade took effect. **US Central Command** confirmed redirecting commercial vessels attempting to breach the blockade since operations resumed. These developments have led to increased global oil prices and prompted concerns among countries like **India**, which relies on the **Strait of Hormuz** for energy and trade, highlighting the region's volatility. The ongoing conflict has also affected markets, with **Bitcoin** slipping due to heightened geopolitical tensions.

## European Security and Ukraine

In **Europe**, the focus remains on bolstering defense capabilities and addressing the ongoing conflict in **Ukraine**. A ten-nation coalition, including the **UK**, **Germany**, and **France**, has launched an initiative to develop an independent ballistic missile defense architecture, positioned as a more affordable alternative to **US Patriot** systems. This move signals an accelerating trend of **European** defense-industrial decoupling from **Washington**, partly attributed to perceived **NATO** ambivalence from **President Trump**. However, details regarding financing mechanisms and industrial-base timelines for this initiative remain unclear.

The conflict in **Ukraine** continues to see significant developments. **Ukraine's** long-range drone campaign has reportedly achieved strategic effect, reducing **Russian** refining capacity to a 21-year low. This has led **Moscow** to summon **Germany's** ambassador over alleged Western complicity, indicating the economic pressure this campaign is exerting on **Russia's** fiscal stability and war financing. Reports also suggest that **Russia's** mass mobilization model is facing diminishing returns on the battlefield, with an increase in troops not necessarily translating to enhanced combat effectiveness.

Diplomatically, the **European Union** made significant strides in its enlargement process on July 14, 2026, by holding accession conferences with four candidate countries: **Ukraine**, **Montenegro**, **Moldova**, and **Albania**. These meetings marked important milestones, with **Ukraine** and **Moldova** opening negotiations on a new thematic cluster, and **Montenegro** and **Albania** provisionally closing individual negotiating chapters. The **UK Parliament's Foreign Affairs Committee** has also launched an inquiry into **Russia**, aiming to define the **UK's** long-term strategic approach to a post-conflict **Russia** and examine **Russia's** international alliances, domestic situation, and economic sustainability.

## African Diplomatic Shifts

In the **Sahel** region, a notable diplomatic reconciliation has occurred between **Algeria** and **Mali**, with **Russia** appearing to benefit from the improved relations. On July 10, 2026, **Algeria** reopened its airspace to civilian and military aircraft traveling to and from **Mali**, and **Bamako** introduced reciprocal measures. Both governments also agreed to reinstate their ambassadors, ending a 15-month diplomatic confrontation that began after **Algeria** destroyed a **Turkish**-made **Malian** reconnaissance drone in March 2025, claiming it violated its territory.

This restoration of diplomatic ties brings two of **Russia's** key African security partners back into cooperation, further cementing **Moscow's** expanding influence across the **Sahel**. **Russia** has reportedly facilitated the repair of relations through discreet negotiations involving African leaders aligned with **Moscow**, leveraging its central position in both countries' defense and foreign policy calculations. Both **Algeria** and **Mali** are significant recipients of **Russian** military equipment, with **Algeria** being one of **Moscow's** oldest African defense partners and **Mali's** ties rapidly expanding following recent military coups. This development comes as **Washington** seeks to regain influence in **Bamako** after **Mali's** military government expelled **French** troops and deepened its relationship with **Russia**.
