The global geopolitical landscape remains highly volatile, dominated by the ongoing **US-Israeli** military operations against **Iran** and **Iran's** retaliatory actions across the Middle East. This conflict has led to significant casualties, widespread destruction, and a severe disruption of global energy markets due to the effective closure of the **Strait of Hormuz**. Concurrently, **US President Donald Trump's** foreign policy rhetoric continues to shape international alliances and economic strategies, particularly concerning **NATO** and global energy supplies.

## Escalation in the Middle East and Global Repercussions

Hostilities in the Middle East have sharply escalated since joint **Israeli-US** strikes on **Iran** began on February 28, prompting retaliatory strikes from **Iran** targeting **Israel**, **US** bases, and allied assets across the region. Reports indicate thousands of casualties, with the **US-based rights group HRANA** stating 3,492 people have been killed in **Iran**, including 1,574 civilians and at least 236 children. The **International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies** reported at least 1,900 killed and 20,000 injured in **Iran** from these strikes. In **Israel**, 19 people have been killed by missiles launched from **Iran** and **Lebanon**, and 10 **Israeli** soldiers died in southern **Lebanon**. Tragically, three **United Nations** peacekeepers from **Indonesia** were also killed in separate incidents in southern **Lebanon**.

The targets of the **US** and **Israeli** operations included **Iran's** nuclear infrastructure, ballistic missile facilities, naval assets, and major steel facilities, resulting in the deaths of key figures such as **Iranian Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei** and **Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council Ali Larijani**. **Iran's** retaliatory strikes have similarly hit critical infrastructure and civilian objects. An **Iranian** drone attack recently struck fuel tanks at **Kuwait International Airport**, causing a large fire.

A critical development is the effective closure of the **Strait of Hormuz** by **Iran**, a vital waterway through which one-fifth of the world's oil supply typically passes. This blockade is being described as the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market". In response, **US President Donald Trump** indicated that the **US** military campaign against **Iran** could conclude within two to three weeks, emphasizing that **Tehran** does not need a deal but must be "put into the stone ages" without the ability to acquire a nuclear weapon. He also urged **Iran** to make a deal following a recent bridge strike.

Internationally, there are calls for de-escalation. **China** and **Pakistan** have put forward a five-point peace plan advocating for an immediate ceasefire, renewed negotiations, protection of infrastructure, and the reopening of the **Strait of Hormuz**. The **United Arab Emirates** is reportedly lobbying for a **UN Security Council** resolution to authorize a coalition to forcibly reopen the strait. The **UN Security Council** previously adopted resolution 2817, which condemned **Iran's** strikes and demanded an immediate halt to attacks. Meanwhile, **France** and **Italy** have expressed reservations about some **US-Israeli** military operations.

On the **Palestinian** issue, **UN** experts have warned of "irreparable harm" in **Jerusalem** and ongoing "genocide" in **Gaza** and the **West Bank**. **Israel's** recent adoption of a law making the death penalty the default sentence for Palestinians convicted of lethal attacks has drawn international condemnation.

## Energy Markets and Shifting Alliances

The closure of the **Strait of Hormuz** has had a profound impact on global energy markets, with oil prices surging significantly. Brent crude briefly rose above $120 per barrel, and WTI increased to over $110 per barrel, with **Iran** projecting prices could reach $200 per barrel. Major oil-producing countries in the Middle East have reduced their output due to safety concerns and logistical challenges.

In an effort to stabilize global oil prices, the **United States** has announced the release of 172 million barrels from its Strategic Petroleum Reserve over the next four months, with **France** and the **United Kingdom** also planning releases from their reserves. Additionally, the **Trump** administration has temporarily eased some sanctions on **Russian** oil trade, allowing countries like **India** to purchase millions of barrels, and issued a general license for limited sales of **Iranian** oil currently at sea until April 19. This move aims to alleviate supply tightness, though the **European Union** continues its sanctions on **Russian** oil.

The conflict has also amplified discussions around **US** alliances. **President Trump** has openly questioned the value of **NATO**, suggesting he is "absolutely" considering withdrawing the **United States** from the alliance due to what he perceives as a lack of support from **European** allies in the **Iran** war. **Secretary of State Marco Rubio** reiterated that while **President Trump** supports **NATO**, he expects **European** nations to increase their defense spending. This stance highlights ongoing divisions between **Washington** and some of its traditional partners over the conduct and burden-sharing of military operations.

## Regional Dynamics in Europe and Africa

In **Europe**, the geopolitical landscape is marked by ongoing tensions with **Russia**. **Ukraine** has extended its sanctions lists, while **Russia's Central Bank** has initiated a lawsuit against the **European Union** over frozen assets. There are also concerns that **Russia** may intensify its "shadow war" tactics against **Europe**, potentially targeting infrastructure and attempting to disrupt democratic processes, particularly in upcoming elections. **Hungary's Prime Minister Viktor Orbán** is facing an electoral challenge in April, a contest drawing attention amid broader concerns about democratic backsliding in some **European** nations.

**Africa** continues to navigate a complex environment characterized by converging conflicts, economic challenges, and increasing geopolitical competition from external powers seeking strategic advantages related to minerals and trade routes. Several African nations are preparing for elections in 2026, many within fragile and polarized contexts, including **Benin** and **Djibouti** in April. In **Senegal**, **President Bassirou Diomaye Faye** recently signed a bill that doubles the maximum prison term for same-sex sexual acts, reflecting a trend of similar legislation in several **African** countries.
