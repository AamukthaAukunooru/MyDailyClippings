## Middle East Tensions Escalate

Hostilities between the **United States** and **Iran** have intensified following an attack in **Jordan** that killed two American soldiers and left another missing. This incident marked the first U.S. troops to die from hostile fire since an April truce. In response, **U.S. forces** initiated retaliatory strikes early Sunday. Officials have indicated that **Jordan** is becoming a new focus in the conflict, with four Iranian attacks targeting **U.S. troops** in the country over five days, including the fatal strike on Friday. The deaths bring the overall American toll in the war with **Iran** to 16, leading **President Trump** to vow vengeance. Broader concerns have also been raised regarding the targeting of civilian infrastructure, such as bridges, power plants, and desalination facilities by both **U.S.** and **Iranian forces**, with some analysts suggesting these actions could be considered war crimes.

## European Political Shifts and Regional Conflicts

This development marks a significant political change in the country. Concurrently, in **Germany**, **Jens Spahn**, a prominent figure in the conservative **Christian Democratic Union (CDU)**, resigned amid controversy surrounding his use of a surrogate mother in the **U.S.** to have a child. Surrogacy is not permitted in **Germany**, and the **CDU** officially opposes its legalization, leading to criticism given **Spahn's** prior stance against it.
