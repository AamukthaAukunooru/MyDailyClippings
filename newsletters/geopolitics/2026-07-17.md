My Daily Clippings

The past 24-48 hours have seen a significant escalation in geopolitical tensions, primarily driven by intensified exchanges between the **United States** and **Iran** in the Middle East. Concurrently, the conflict in **Ukraine** continues to evolve with new attacks and diplomatic engagements, while a notable geopolitical realignment is underway in West Africa. These developments highlight a complex and interconnected global security landscape, influencing energy markets, regional alliances, and international stability.

## Escalation in the Middle East and Global Energy Concerns

Tensions in the Middle East have reached a critical point with the **United States** intensifying its military strikes against **Iran** over the past two days, marking the fifth consecutive day of such operations. US Central Command has reported strikes targeting **Iranian** command centers, air defense sites, and missile and drone capabilities, with some operations extending to areas closer to **Tehran** and in northern **Iran**. These actions follow the reimposition of a naval blockade on **Iranian** ports by the **US military**, and a strike on an oil tanker accused of attempting to breach this blockade. **President Trump** has reportedly considered expanding US military operations, including potential ground forces or bombing a site at Pickaxe Mountain for covert nuclear work, though no final decision has been made. He has also threatened to target **Iranian** bridges and power plants next week unless negotiations resume, while dropping plans to collect a 20% fee on ships passing through the **Strait of Hormuz**.

In response, **Iran** has launched retaliatory missile and drone strikes targeting **US** allies in the region, specifically **Jordan**, **Bahrain**, and **Kuwait**. **Iranian** officials reported that overnight strikes by the **US** killed at least seven military personnel and wounded 260 across the country. The **Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)** has threatened to close "all other export corridors that benefit the **US** and its allies," emphasizing that regional energy exports are "either shared by all or denied to all". Analysts suggest **Iran** may leverage its **Houthi** allies in **Yemen** to close the **Bab el-Mandeb** gateway to the Red Sea, posing a significant threat to global energy supplies. This ongoing conflict has already impacted global commodity markets, particularly for plastics and metals, with prices showing volatility based on the prospects of de-escalation.

Separately, in the broader Middle East, **Israeli** airstrikes in **Gaza** have resulted in multiple fatalities, including a family, with **Palestinian** health officials reporting at least a dozen deaths over the past two days. **Israel** continues to assert its control over significant portions of the **Gaza Strip**.

## European Security Dynamics and the Russia-Ukraine War

The conflict between **Russia** and **Ukraine** remains a central point of concern in Europe. **Russia** launched missile attacks on **Kyiv** overnight, resulting in two deaths and six injuries, including a 16-year-old boy. Warehouses and non-residential buildings were hit, and emergency crews battled fires after the assault on the capital. This attack occurred hours after **European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen** visited **Kyiv** and announced a new drone deal between **Ukraine** and the **European Union**, aimed at combining **Ukrainian** battlefield expertise with **Europe's** industrial capacity for joint production. Meanwhile, the **UK Prime Minister** met with **President Zelenskyy** in **Kyiv** on July 16, reaffirming the **UK's** unwavering support for **Ukraine** in its fight against **Russia's** "brutal regime". **Ukrainian** forces have also continued to target **Russian** vessels, reporting strikes on at least 11 **Russian** vessels in the Black Sea and 116 in the Sea of Azov this month.

In a broader European security context, a recent **Ankara Summit** communique regarding **NATO's Article 5** has been interpreted by some analyses as masking a structural division within the alliance. This split is perceived to be between **US**-aligned factions and those advocating for greater European autonomy, impacting burden-sharing, **Ukraine's** security guarantees, and **Europe's** ability to deter **Russia** without **US** backing. Frontline states such as the **Baltics** and **Poland** are reportedly diverging from Western **Europe** on the urgency and resourcing of defense. On a diplomatic front, the **EU** held accession conferences on July 14 with four candidate countries, **Ukraine**, **Montenegro**, **Moldova**, and **Albania**, marking a significant step forward in the EU's enlargement process.

## Shifting Alliances in West Africa and the Sahel

A notable geopolitical reordering is taking place across West Africa and the Sahel region. **Burkina Faso** has formally severed diplomatic ties with **France**, aligning itself with **Russia**, **Turkey**, and **China**. This move follows similar realignments by **Mali** and **Niger**, indicating a significant collapse of **French** influence in the region. The operational role of **Wagner/Africa Corps** successor forces in propping up the junta in **Burkina Faso** has been noted, raising concerns about the vacuum created and its potential effect on jihadist expansion into coastal West Africa. This shift underscores a broader trend of African nations diversifying their security and economic partnerships beyond traditional Western allies.
