## Middle East Tensions and Diplomatic Efforts

A major diplomatic initiative is currently unfolding in **Islamabad**, **Pakistan**, where foreign ministers from **Saudi Arabia**, **Turkey**, and **Egypt** are meeting on March 29-30. The quadrilateral talks aim to facilitate de-escalation between the **United States** and **Iran**, which have been engaged in a month-long conflict. **Pakistan's** Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister, **Mohammad Ishaq Dar**, is chairing the session, underscoring **Islamabad's** increasingly active role in regional diplomacy. Discussions are expected to focus on containing tensions and promoting political solutions amid heightened concerns for regional stability.

The conflict has seen strong rhetoric from both sides. **Iran's** parliament speaker, **Mohammad Bagher Qalibaf**, issued a stark warning against a **US** ground invasion, stating that **US** troops would be "set on fire". **Iran** also threatened to consider **Israeli** universities and branches of **US** universities in the region as "legitimate targets" unless Iranian universities are guaranteed safety from **Israeli** airstrikes. These warnings follow **US** and **Israeli** strikes on Iranian military and energy infrastructure, and subsequent Iranian attacks on **Israel** and neighboring Gulf Arab states.

The humanitarian and economic consequences of the conflict are significant. Over 3,000 people have been killed in the conflict, with casualty figures reported in **Iran**, **Israel**, **Lebanon**, **Iraq**, and other Gulf states. The **UN** and humanitarian organizations report an intensifying crisis, with mass displacement, particularly in **Lebanon**, and severe strains on civilian infrastructure and access to essential services like food, clean water, and medical care across the **Middle East**. Economically, the conflict has disrupted global oil supplies and shipping routes, especially the **Strait of Hormuz**, leading to a surge in oil and jet fuel prices. The potential involvement of **Yemen's Houthis** also raises concerns about further disruptions to shipping in the **Bab el-Mandeb Strait**. The **United States** has deployed additional forces, including elements of the 31st Marine Expeditionary Unit, to the region, with **President Trump** expressing a desire to conclude the war within four to six weeks.

## Escalation in Ukraine and International Justice Efforts

The conflict in **Ukraine** continues to witness intense military activity. On March 28, **Russia** launched a series of air attacks across **Ukraine**, resulting in at least four fatalities and damaging critical infrastructure. Targets included a port and a maternity hospital in the southern city of **Odesa**, where two people were killed and at least 13 wounded. In response, **Ukrainian** forces have continued their long-range strike campaign, targeting **Russian** defense industrial and oil infrastructure. This has reportedly led **Russia** to prepare for a temporary suspension of all gasoline exports starting April 1, likely in an attempt to address domestic price increases exacerbated by these strikes.

**Ukraine's Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov** indicated that **Russian** losses are increasing, suggesting that March 2026 could see a record number of casualties for **Russia**. He attributed this rise to **Russia's** strategic decisions, the deployment of unprepared personnel, a lack of evacuation procedures, and constant pressure from **Ukrainian** drones. **Fedorov** further noted that reaching a monthly figure of 50,000 destroyed soldiers would have catastrophic consequences for **Russia**.

In a move towards accountability, the **European Union** is advancing its efforts to establish a **Special Tribunal for the Crime of Aggression against Ukraine**. On March 26, the **European Commission** adopted a proposal to initiate the process for the **EU** to become a founding member of this tribunal. Once approved by Member States, the **EU** aims to play a central role in the tribunal's operations, including membership in its Management Committee, with the mandate to prosecute senior political and military leaders for the crime of aggression against **Ukraine**. This initiative is being set up within the framework of the **Council of Europe**, following an agreement signed with **Ukraine** in June 2025.
