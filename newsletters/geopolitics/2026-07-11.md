The global geopolitical landscape has seen a significant escalation in tensions between the **United States** and **Iran** over the past 48 hours, particularly in the **Strait of Hormuz**, following the collapse of a fragile ceasefire. Concurrently, the conflict in **Ukraine** continues to intensify with new offensives, while the recent **NATO** summit in **Ankara**, **Türkiye**, revealed ongoing internal discussions within the alliance. Developments in **East Asia** also underscore shifting regional dynamics.

## Middle East Tensions: US-Iran Escalation and Diplomatic Efforts

A fragile ceasefire between the **United States** and **Iran** has reportedly collapsed, leading to a renewed exchange of military strikes in the **Persian Gulf** region. On Wednesday, the **US** launched significant operations, striking approximately 90 **Iranian** military targets, including air defense systems, coastal surveillance assets, missile and drone storage sites, and naval capabilities along **Iran's** coastline. These actions followed alleged **Iranian** attacks on at least three commercial vessels transiting the **Strait of Hormuz**. **US President Donald Trump** declared the ceasefire "over" and warned of stronger military action if **Iran** continued to target shipping or **US** forces.

In retaliation, **Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)** announced missile and drone strikes targeting **US** military facilities in **Kuwait** and **Bahrain**, prompting air raid sirens and defensive interceptions in the **Gulf** states. **Kuwait** reported shooting down two ballistic missiles and 13 drones, with one casualty from falling debris. **Bahrain** also confirmed intercepting incoming fire. **Qatar** and **Jordan** also activated precautionary measures amid fears of wider conflict.

Regional mediators, including **Qatar** and **Pakistan**, have engaged in multiple phone calls with both **US** and **Iranian** officials in an attempt to de-escalate tensions and revive negotiations on a nuclear deal. Technical-level talks, scheduled for July 11 in **Islamabad**, have not been canceled, with mediators working to keep the diplomatic track alive despite the recent escalation. These discussions are expected to address core issues such as **Iran's** nuclear program, sanctions relief, frozen assets, and freedom of navigation in **Hormuz**.

Adding another layer of complexity, **Israel** reportedly shared new intelligence with the **United States** indicating a fresh **Iranian** plan to assassinate **President Trump**. This warning, which arrived this week, has been noted by **US** officials, though independent verification is still pending. The escalation coincides with the ongoing funeral processions for **Iran's** late **Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei**, who was reportedly killed in earlier strikes.

## Eastern Europe: Ukraine-Russia Conflict and NATO's Role

The conflict between **Ukraine** and **Russia** has seen renewed intensity, particularly with **Ukrainian** drone operations. **Ukraine's** military has intensified its attacks near **Russian**-annexed **Crimea**, with reports of at least 25 ships being hit and set on fire in the **Sea of Azov** in the past four days. **Ukrainian** forces have also continued their barrage of attacks against **Russian** oil infrastructure, striking targets in multiple regions, including the **Ilsky Oil Refinery** in southern **Russia**.

In response, **Russia** has maintained its offensive, with **Russian** strikes reportedly killing at least 265 civilians and injuring 1,816 in **Ukraine** in June, marking the highest combined casualty count since February 2022. **Russian President Vladimir Putin** is reportedly rejecting calls to negotiate peace with **Kyiv**, with sources close to the **Kremlin** indicating that **Putin** is resolved to continue fighting and may escalate the conflict, believing **Russia** will soon capture the **Donbas** region. **Russia** has also moved to halt diesel exports until the end of July in an attempt to shield its energy markets from the impact of the war and recent drone attacks.

During the **NATO** summit in **Ankara**, **Türkiye**, held on July 7-8, **President Trump** announced that the **US** would grant **Ukraine** the right to manufacture **Patriot** air defense missiles. This decision comes as **Ukraine** has repeatedly emphasized its need for more **Patriot** missiles to counter **Russia's** ballistic missile attacks. The summit also served as a "stress test" for alliance cohesion, exposing transatlantic divisions over defense spending, support for **Ukraine**, and **US** security commitments. **Russia** denounced **NATO's** decisions at the summit, warning of potentially catastrophic consequences.

## East Asia and Global Alliances

In **East Asia**, the **European Parliament** recently passed a resolution advocating for closer cooperation with partners in the region, explicitly mentioning **Taiwan** 44 times. The measure, passed on Tuesday with broad support, pledged to reject "any coercive unilateral change of the status quo" in the **Taiwan Strait**. It described **Taiwan** as a "reliable key democratic partner" sharing common values with the **EU**, including democracy, the rule of law, and human rights. The resolution also urged diplomatic efforts to prevent further escalation in the **Taiwan Strait**, as well as the **East** and **South China Seas**, and called for contingency plans for scenarios such as a blockade of **Taiwan** by the **Chinese** navy.

This development follows a **Chinese** submarine-launched ballistic missile test into the **Pacific** on July 6, which was its first such test into international waters and underscored regional concerns about notification protocols. Meanwhile, **China** and **Bangladesh** have upgraded their bilateral relations to a "community with a shared future in the new era," following an official visit by **Bangladesh's Prime Minister Tarique Rahman** to **China** in late June. The two nations agreed to deepen cooperation across various sectors, including connectivity, scientific and technological innovation, green energy, water resources management, and defense.
