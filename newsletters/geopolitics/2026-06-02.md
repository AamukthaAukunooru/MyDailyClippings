The global geopolitical landscape remains highly dynamic, with significant developments unfolding across multiple regions. Tensions in the Middle East have escalated sharply, marked by a major Israeli offensive into southern **Lebanon** and renewed military exchanges between the **United States** and **Iran**, further complicating already fragile diplomatic efforts. Meanwhile, the conflict in **Ukraine** continues to see shifts in tactics, and concerns regarding global economic security are rising as major powers engage in economic competition.

## Middle East Escalation and US-Iran Tensions

The Middle East is experiencing a significant intensification of hostilities, particularly along the **Israel-Lebanon** border and involving **US-Iran** relations. **Israel** has launched a large-scale ground offensive into southern **Lebanon**, with its forces crossing the **Litani River** and capturing the strategically important **Beaufort Castle**. This marks the deepest Israeli incursion into **Lebanon** since 1982. **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** described the capture as a "dramatic shift" in the campaign against **Hezbollah** and has reportedly ordered operations to deepen and expand, potentially including strikes in **Beirut's Dahiyeh** district. This offensive is widely seen as nullifying previous ceasefire agreements and indicating a strategy to establish a permanent buffer zone. In response, **Hezbollah** has launched rocket and drone attacks targeting northern **Israel**. The escalation prompted **France** to request an emergency meeting of the **UN Security Council**, which has since convened to address the situation in **Lebanon**. The **UN Secretary-General António Guterres**'s spokesman, **Stephane Dujarric**, expressed deep alarm over the escalating military activities and emphasized that there is "no alternative to a diplomatic solution".

Concurrently, diplomatic efforts to secure a broader ceasefire between the **United States** and **Iran** and ensure the reopening of the **Strait of Hormuz** have faced considerable setbacks. Reports indicate that a proposed deal, which aimed to extend a ceasefire and facilitate shipping through the vital strait, is currently "in flux". **US President Donald Trump** reportedly requested amendments to the draft agreement, seeking tougher conditions on **Iran's** nuclear commitments and the terms for reopening the **Strait of Hormuz**. Following the Israeli escalation in **Lebanon**, **Iran** announced it had suspended negotiations with the **United States** and reiterated threats to reseal the **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical global energy corridor. The **US** recently sanctioned **Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA)**, an entity reportedly linked to the **Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)** and formed to formalize a toll system in the strait. Military exchanges between the two nations have also been reported, with the **US** carrying out "self-defense" strikes against **Iranian** radar and command and control sites after an **American MQ-1 drone** was shot down over international waters. **Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps** claimed to have retaliated by striking a **US** air base. **Kuwait** also reported intercepting missile and drone attacks. The ongoing uncertainty has led to volatility in global oil prices, with **Brent crude** approaching the **US$100** mark. Within the region, there are concerns that a **US-Iran** agreement could shift **Israel's** focus entirely to **Gaza**, potentially intensifying military pressure on **Hamas** if the Strip remains outside the scope of any deal.

## Ukraine-Russia Conflict Update

The conflict between **Ukraine** and **Russia** continues to evolve, with **Ukraine** reportedly gaining tactical advantages through innovative warfare. Reports indicate that **Ukraine** has leveraged advanced drone technology to break a prolonged battlefield stalemate, outflanking **Russian** forces and reclaiming territory. In response to these developments, **Moscow** has intensified its aerial strikes and issued regional threats. **Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** has claimed that approximately 40% of **Russian** refining capacity has been taken offline due to these drone attacks, which have also targeted **Russian** oil facilities and supply routes. Authorities in **Russian-controlled Crimea** have reportedly implemented fuel rationing following disruptions to supply lines.

## Global Economic Security and Trade

Concerns over global economic security are increasingly shaping international relations, particularly in the context of **Europe's** economic ties with **China**. Analysts suggest that **Europe** is experiencing a "China shock" of historic proportions, driven by **China's** state-driven industrial policies and significant overcapacity in various sectors, including steel, photovoltaics, electric vehicles, and batteries. This situation highlights a broader trend where economic interdependence is increasingly being used as an instrument of power by major global actors. **Beijing** is seen as weaponizing overcapacity and export dependencies, while **Washington** is employing market access, tariffs, and financial leverage to pursue reindustrialization and geopolitical advantage. In response to these pressures and the normalization of economic coercion, **Europe** is reportedly shifting towards a doctrine of "reactive assertiveness," recognizing the need for credible retaliation to deter such actions. This emerging approach suggests a fundamental change in **Europe's** economic strategy, moving away from past assumptions of a purely rules-based system.

Additionally, in Asia, **India** has continued to strengthen its defense partnerships. **India** has finalized a deal to supply **BrahMos** missiles to **Vietnam** and is reportedly in the final stages of a similar agreement with **Indonesia**. These developments underscore ongoing efforts by regional powers to enhance their defense capabilities and strategic alliances.
