My Daily Clippings

A significant development has emerged in the Middle East with the announcement of a tentative peace deal between the **United States** and **Iran**, aiming to conclude the ongoing conflict and reopen the critical **Strait of Hormuz**. This agreement, if finalized, carries substantial implications for regional stability and global energy markets. Meanwhile, other enduring conflicts, notably in **Ukraine** and between **Israel** and **Hamas**, continue to see active hostilities and diplomatic efforts. World leaders are also convening at the **G7 Summit** in **France**, where the **Iran** deal and the **Russia**-**Ukraine** war are prominent topics.

## **US-Iran Peace Deal and the Strait of Hormuz**

**President Trump** announced a deal to end the war with **Iran** and lift the **U.S.** naval blockade, with a signing tentatively scheduled for Friday in **Switzerland**. The agreement is expected to lead to the immediate resumption of shipping traffic in the **Strait of Hormuz**. This waterway has seen a near standstill of maritime traffic since the conflict began, leading to concerns about global energy prices and supply chain disruptions. The proposed deal reportedly includes a 60-day ceasefire and the potential release of $24 billion to **Iran**.

While **President Trump** celebrated the agreement, stating "We ended the war in **Iran** today," and claiming **Iran's** Supreme Leader had approved it, **Iranian** foreign ministry spokesperson **Esmaeil Baghaei** indicated that **Iran** had not reached a final decision on an agreement. Reports from Qatari mediators and **Iranian** officials suggested an agreed-upon text was reached, but final approval from **Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei** was still pending. Hardliners in **Iran** have expressed anger over the terms, with some protesting against a deal they perceive as capitulation.

The **United Kingdom** and **Australia**, during their Ministerial Consultations (AUKMIN) on June 10, 2026, supported the ceasefire between **Iran** and the **US** and welcomed ongoing mediation efforts. They reiterated calls for the permanent reopening of the **Strait of Hormuz** and affirmed that **Iran** must never acquire a nuclear weapon.

## **Ongoing Regional Conflicts and Tensions**

Despite the broader efforts for peace between the **US** and **Iran**, other conflicts in the Middle East persist. The **Israel**-**Hamas** war continues, with recent **Israeli** airstrikes reportedly killing three people in **Gaza**. Mediators, including **Egypt**, **Qatar**, and **Turkey**, have been holding talks with **Hamas** regarding the implementation of a second phase of a **Gaza** plan, which would involve **Hamas** disarming and **Israel** withdrawing forces. A senior **Hamas** official, **Hussam Badran**, noted "real progress" in these talks but urged mediators to compel **Israel** to cease truce violations.

The **Hezbollah**-**Israel** conflict also remains active. **Israel** reportedly restarted strikes on **Tyre**, **Lebanon**, despite a truce, and the **Lebanese** health ministry reported an **Israeli** attack on the **Dahieh** suburb of **Beirut** that killed three people and injured 16. **President Trump** condemned **Israeli** strikes on **Beirut**, urging all parties to show restraint and prevent disruption to the **US-Iran** peace process.

In **Eastern Europe**, the **Russia**-**Ukraine** war continues. **Russia** has escalated air raids on major **Ukrainian** cities, which some experts suggest are intended to distract from **Ukrainian** long-range attacks targeting **Russian** oil production and weapon manufacturing. Recent reports indicate **Russia** launched numerous ballistic missiles into **Ukraine** on the night of June 14-15, following a smaller wave of attacks earlier. **Ukrainian** long-range drones have struck targets deep within **Russia**, including an oil terminal in **St. Petersburg**.

## **Global Diplomatic Engagements and Economic Outlook**

The **G7 Summit** is currently underway in **Evian**, **France**, with world leaders, including **President Trump**, discussing the **Iran** peace deal, **Russia's** war against **Ukraine**, and artificial intelligence. The global economic impact of the **Iran** conflict and the disruption to the **Strait of Hormuz** has been a significant concern, with warnings from oil industry executives about surging energy prices if inventories continue to drain. The potential reopening of the **Strait of Hormuz** is therefore seen as crucial for stabilizing global energy supplies.

Other diplomatic activities include a new defense and security treaty signed between the **UK** and **Poland**, aimed at improving border security, tackling organized crime, and deepening defense cooperation with the **European Union**. Additionally, **Slovenia's** new conservative government abolished an entry ban on **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** and two of his ministers, lifted a ban on imports from **Jewish** settlements, and ended an embargo on military weapons and equipment to and from **Israel**. The **United States** also plans to significantly reduce the aircraft and warships it makes available for **NATO** operations in **Europe**, a decision communicated to allies in early June.
