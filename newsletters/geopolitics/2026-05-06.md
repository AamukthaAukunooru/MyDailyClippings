## Escalating Tensions in the Middle East and the Strait of Hormuz

The **Strait of Hormuz** remains a focal point of international concern, with **US President Donald Trump** announcing "Project Freedom" to guide commercial vessels through the strategic waterway starting May 6. This initiative aims to assist ships stranded in the **Persian Gulf** due to heightened tensions. **Iran** has strongly rejected "Project Freedom," warning that any **American** involvement in managing transit through the strait would violate the existing ceasefire framework. **Iranian** officials have stated that the security of **Hormuz** is under **Iranian** control and warned that **US** actions could be seen as breaking the ceasefire.

Recent days have seen further escalation, including reports of a tanker being struck by unknown projectiles in waters north of **Fujairah** in the **United Arab Emirates (UAE)**. **Iran** has reportedly expanded its targeting to **UAE** infrastructure, with missile and drone strikes on the **Fujairah** petroleum facility, a critical hub for oil storage and ship-to-ship fuel transfers. These actions have been described as a qualitative shift in the conflict's geographic scope. In a notable development, the **UAE** formalized its departure from the Organization of Arab Petroleum Exporting Countries (**OAPEC**) on May 4, 2026, following earlier withdrawals from **OPEC** and **OPEC+** effective May 1. This repositioning by **Abu Dhabi** is seen as a strategic response to the ongoing conflict and further undermines **OPEC's** market influence.

The crisis has significantly impacted global energy markets, with Brent crude surging to $114 per barrel on May 5, 2026, before settling around $110 per barrel. The broader forward curve indicates market conviction that structural supply constraints will persist into 2027, even with a diplomatic resolution. Efforts to find a diplomatic solution continue, but **President Trump** has stated that **Iran's** 14-point proposal to end the war is "not acceptable".

## Russia-Ukraine Conflict: Ceasefire Proposals and Continued Hostilities

The conflict between **Russia** and **Ukraine** saw a significant diplomatic maneuver as **Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** declared a unilateral ceasefire for the night of May 5-6. This declaration was a direct response to **Russian** rhetoric regarding a May 9 "Victory Day" ceasefire, with **Zelenskyy** urging **Russian** forces to reciprocate and emphasizing that human life is paramount. **Ukrainian Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha** stated that this ceasefire proposal is a serious offer to end the war and transition to diplomacy, and that May 6 will reveal **Russia's** true intentions.

In response to **Zelenskyy's** proposal and earlier remarks about potential **Ukrainian** drones over **Moscow**, **Russia's** defense ministry issued threats of massive missile strikes on central **Kyiv** if **Ukraine** did not agree to its own proposed May 8-9 ceasefire. Despite **Ukraine's** ceasefire declaration, **Russia** carried out missile and drone strikes on the night of May 5, targeting energy facilities, oil and gas infrastructure, railways, and residential buildings in regions such as **Dnipropetrovsk**, **Poltava**, and **Kharkiv**. These attacks resulted in casualties, with five people killed and dozens injured, prompting **Zelenskyy** to label the actions as "absolute cynicism".

According to the **Institute for the Study of War (ISW)**, **Russian** forces experienced a net loss of territory in the **Ukrainian** theater in April 2026, marking the first time this has occurred since **Ukraine's** August 2024 incursion into **Kursk Oblast**. The **ISW** also noted a relative decline in the rate of **Russian** advances, potentially influenced by seasonal weather patterns and **Ukrainian** counterattacks.

## Asia-Pacific Security and Global Trade Dynamics

In the **Asia-Pacific**, military cooperation and evolving trade relations are shaping the regional geopolitical landscape. The **United States**, **Philippines**, and **Japan** are conducting counter-landing live-fire drills in **Laoag** as part of the ongoing **Balikatan** exercise, testing interoperability with advanced weapon systems like HIMARS rocket systems, Stinger missiles, and drones. This exercise underscores efforts to strengthen regional security alliances amid broader geopolitical concerns.

Economically, **Japan** and **Vietnam** have agreed to deepen cooperation in critical sectors such as energy, minerals, artificial intelligence, semiconductors, and space, aiming to mitigate risks posed by disruptions to global supply chains. Meanwhile, tensions persist in the **South China Sea**, with **China** and the **Philippines** exchanging accusations.

Broader trade tensions are also escalating on the global stage. **US President Trump** has threatened to impose additional tariffs on **EU**-imported cars and trucks, with rates potentially rising to 25%, citing the **EU's** alleged failure to fulfill a previously agreed trade deal. This move is expected to increase global manufacturing trade costs and accelerate the restructuring of automotive supply chains. **China** has also ordered companies to disregard **US** sanctions, further highlighting the ongoing trade friction between the two economic powers.
