My Daily Clippings

Geopolitical developments over the past 24-48 hours have been dominated by escalating tensions in the Middle East, with the fragile ceasefire between the **United States** and **Iran** facing significant challenges. Despite reciprocal military actions, diplomatic efforts are underway to de-escalate the situation. Concurrently, security concerns in the **Indo-Pacific** continue to grow amidst **China's** expanding military posture, while the **European Union** has advanced key trade agreements and is preparing for a new Council presidency.

## Middle East: Ceasefire Under Strain Amid Renewed Strikes

The interim ceasefire between the **United States** and **Iran**, signed on **June 17**, has been severely tested by a series of tit-for-tat military actions over the weekend. **U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM)** reported conducting strikes on **Iranian** military targets on **June 27**, including missile and drone sites, in response to an **Iranian** drone attack on the commercial tanker M/T Kiku near the **Strait of Hormuz**. The **Panama**-flagged vessel was reportedly transiting the strategic waterway carrying over two million barrels of crude oil. **CENTCOM** stated that **Iran** had been given a chance to honor the ceasefire but "elected not to".

In retaliation, **Iran's Revolutionary Guard** claimed to have launched missile and drone strikes targeting eight **U.S.** military sites in **Kuwait** and **Bahrain** in the early hours of **June 28**. These strikes reportedly hit "key **US** military infrastructures," including the **Ali Al Salem Air Base** in **Kuwait** and the headquarters of the **US Fifth Fleet** in **Bahrain**. **Bahraini** air defenses reportedly intercepted several projectiles, though one attack destroyed a residential building. Several **Arab** countries, including **Saudi Arabia**, **Kuwait**, **Bahrain**, **Jordan**, and the **United Arab Emirates**, have condemned the alleged **Iranian** drone attacks, calling them a violation of sovereignty and a setback to regional de-escalation efforts.

Despite the renewed hostilities, diplomatic channels remain open. High-level emergency meetings between the **United States** and **Iran** are scheduled to take place in **Doha, Qatar**, on **June 30**. **U.S. President Donald Trump** announced that **Iran** had requested this meeting to discuss the **memorandum of understanding (MoU)** aimed at ending four months of active conflict and reopening the **Strait of Hormuz**. However, **Iranian** Deputy Foreign Minister **Kazem Gharibabadi** indicated that technical talks were not yet planned for this week and would only be held "when the conditions are met," without further elaboration. **Iranian** officials have reiterated their intent to assert sovereignty over the strait, with Foreign Minister **Abbas Araghchi** stating on **June 28** that **Iran** is "solely responsible" for its management under the **MoU**, a point that appears to contradict the fifth article of the agreement which calls for dialogue with **Oman** and other **Persian Gulf** littoral states. Meanwhile, **Russian President Vladimir Putin** stated on **June 28** that he expects a team of **U.S.** negotiators in **Moscow** once an agreement is reached with **Iran** over the Middle East conflict.

## Asia-Pacific: Deterrence and Expanding Maritime Presence

In the **Indo-Pacific** region, concerns over **China's** military assertiveness continue to drive security discussions. **Adm. Samuel Paparo**, chief of the **Indo-Pacific Command**, reportedly submitted a private congressional assessment calling for $122 billion in new military spending, which he deems the minimum necessary to deter **China**. The assessment highlights **China's** "aggressive military modernization, territorial expansion, and deepening relationships with **Russia** and **North Korea**" as key challenges. The proposed spending includes $66 billion for new offensive and defensive missiles, as well as $18 billion for counter command and control electronic systems.

**China** has also been observed expanding its maritime law enforcement and research activities in waters east of **Taiwan**. This expansion, which includes deployments of research vessels and **Chinese Coast Guard (CCG)** vessels, may indicate an intent to regularize such operations and potentially alter the status quo by asserting **China's** "near-shore waters" claims. This follows similar patterns seen around **Taiwan's Kinmen Island**. The **Shangri-La Dialogue 2026** in **Singapore** also saw discussions on regional tensions driven by "bloc politics and intensified military activities," with both the **United States** and **China** presenting their visions for regional security.

## European Union: Trade Agreement and Internal Agenda

The **European Union** has taken a significant step in its trade relations with the **United States**, formally approving its tariff commitments under a bilateral trade framework. This approval, made by the **EU Council** on **June 27**, met a **July 4** deadline set by **U.S. President Donald Trump**, who had threatened additional tariffs if ratification was delayed. The agreement includes the elimination of remaining customs duties on **U.S.** industrial goods and expanded preferential access for **American** seafood and non-sensitive agricultural products. The **European Parliament** included a sunset clause, terminating the main preferences on **December 31, 2029**, unless extended, and provisions to suspend benefits if the **U.S.** breaches commitments on steel and aluminum derivatives.

Internally, the **EU** is preparing for the start of the **Irish Council Presidency** on **July 1**. This week, **EU** social affairs ministers are expected to approve Council conclusions on cyber violence against girls and the link between demographic trends and housing policy. The **European Parliament** committees are also preparing for the **July 6-9** plenary session, which will include debates and votes on new legislation concerning air passenger rights, the progress of **Ukraine** and **Moldova** towards **EU** membership, and measures related to rising fertilizer prices. The annual **EU Budget Conference 2026** is also taking place, focusing on leveraging the **EU** budget for strategic priorities and mobilizing additional financial resources.
