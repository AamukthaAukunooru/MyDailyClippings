## The U.S.-Iran Framework Agreement and Regional Stances

A major development has been the electronic signing of a memorandum of understanding between the **United States** and **Iran**, intended to outline a path towards ending their nearly four-month conflict and reopening the **Strait of Hormuz**. **U.S. President Donald Trump** confirmed the deal, with a formal signing ceremony anticipated in **Geneva** on Friday, June 19, 2026. However, interpretations and expectations surrounding the agreement vary significantly among key players. **Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Aragchi** stated that the deal would require **Israel** to withdraw its forces from **Lebanon**, emphasizing that "Without the withdrawal of Israeli forces from the territories they occupied during this war, the war has not fully come to an end". The prospect of the **Strait of Hormuz** reopening has already led to a notable drop in oil prices. **NATO** chief **Mark Rutte** hailed the deal as a "massive step forward" for the reopening of the **Strait of Hormuz**.
