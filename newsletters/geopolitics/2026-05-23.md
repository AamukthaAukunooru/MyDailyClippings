## Middle East: Iran Tensions and Regional Stability

Tensions surrounding **Iran** remain a focal point, with reports indicating that **Tehran** has restarted some drone production and is reconstituting its military capabilities faster than initially estimated during a six-week ceasefire that began in early April. This comes amidst ongoing, indirect ceasefire negotiations between the **United States** and **Iran**, mediated in part by **Pakistan** and **Qatar**. **US President Trump** and **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** reportedly discussed a revised peace memo, with sources suggesting disagreements between the two leaders on the path forward. **President Trump** has issued warnings of potential renewed attacks if **Tehran** does not provide satisfactory responses to the latest **American** offer, while simultaneously indicating a willingness to allow a few more days for a response. In turn, **Iran** has warned that any continued **US** strikes could lead to the conflict spreading "beyond the region".

Economically, **Iran** is reportedly consolidating its control over the strategic **Strait of Hormuz**, enforcing a multi-tiered system for vessel clearance. This comes as the **United Arab Emirates** is nearing 50% completion of a second pipeline designed to bypass the **Strait of Hormuz**, aiming to double its export capacity through **Fujairah**. Domestically, **Iran** is experiencing its longest government-imposed internet blackout, which has lasted nearly three months.

Separately, the humanitarian situation in **Gaza** remains dire, a senior **UN** official warned the **Security Council**, citing delays in implementing a Council-backed peace plan. This period also saw **Israeli Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir** draw international condemnation after posting a video of himself taunting detained pro-Palestinian activists from a **Gaza** aid flotilla. **Prime Minister Netanyahu** subsequently stated that **Ben-Gvir's** actions were not in line with **Israel's** values and ordered the activists' swift deportation. In a related development, the **US Senate** advanced a war powers resolution aimed at limiting **President Trump's** authority to authorize further strikes against **Iran**.

## The Americas: US Pressure on Cuba

The **United States** has significantly ratcheted up pressure on **Cuba**, with the **Justice Department** announcing murder charges against former **Cuban President Raúl Castro** for the 1996 downing of two planes operated by a Cuban exile group, Brothers to the Rescue. This move marks a major escalation in Washington's campaign against the island nation. Concurrently, the **USS Nimitz** aircraft carrier and its escort warships entered the southern **Caribbean Sea**, described by a **US** official as a show of force against **Cuba**. **President Trump** notably declined to rule out the use of military force to extract **Castro** from **Cuba**.

In a separate gesture, **Secretary of State Marco Rubio** offered **Cuba** $100 million in food and medicine, contingent on the aid being distributed through trusted charities such as the **Catholic Church**. These actions collectively reflect a intensified strategy by the **Trump** administration towards **Havana**.

## Emerging Multipolarity: Russia-China Axis

A notable development in international relations was the meeting between **Chinese President Xi Jinping** and **Russian leader Vladimir Putin** in **Beijing**. Following their talks, the two leaders issued a "Joint Declaration of the Russian Federation and the People's Republic of China on the Establishment of a Multipolar World and a New Type of International Relations". In their statements, they warned against a global "law of the jungle" and condemned "treacherous military strikes against other countries," a thinly veiled reference to the **United States'** military campaign against **Iran**.

The joint declaration emphasized a commitment to genuine multilateralism, advocating for a global order characterized by openness, inclusiveness, mutually beneficial cooperation, and the equality and indivisibility of security. This strategic alignment underscores their shared vision for a transformed global governance system and a departure from a unipolar international structure.
