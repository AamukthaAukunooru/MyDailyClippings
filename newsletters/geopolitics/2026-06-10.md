My Daily Clippings

The global geopolitical landscape remains characterized by a complex interplay of regional conflicts and evolving great power dynamics. A fragile de-escalation between **Israel** and **Iran** is shadowed by intensifying proxy conflicts, particularly in **Lebanon**. Meanwhile, the **Russia-Ukraine** conflict continues with escalating long-range strikes from both sides. Tensions persist in the **South China Sea** as maritime disputes deepen, coinciding with a mixed picture in **US-China** relations, marked by both diplomatic engagement and heightened technological competition.

## Middle East Tensions and the Strait of Hormuz

A precarious de-escalation has been observed between **Israel** and **Iran** following a recent exchange of missile strikes. **US President Donald Trump** reportedly urged both sides to "immediately stop shooting," leading to indications from both **Tehran** and **Jerusalem** that direct strikes have halted for now. However, this fragile calm is contrasted by an intensification of conflict in **Southern Lebanon**, where **Israel** has increased operations against **Hezbollah**, killing at least 14 people in recent strikes. **Hezbollah** continues to fire into northern **Israel**, and **Israel** has conducted strikes against southern areas of **Beirut**. **Iran's** foreign minister stated that "no tangible progress" had been made in talks to end the conflict and warned that any attack on **Beirut** as part of **Israel's** campaign against **Hezbollah** could lead to a resumption of the broader **US-Iran** conflict.

The **Strait of Hormuz** remains a critical flashpoint, with maritime traffic experiencing near standstill since the **Iran** war began in late February. Oil industry executives have warned the **US** administration that disruptions in the Strait are draining inventories, potentially leading to surging global energy prices. There are also reports of a helicopter crash near the **Strait of Hormuz**, which **President Trump** has attributed to **Iran**. This situation has prompted Gulf countries exporting oil via the Strait to discuss alternative pipelines. The **UK's** Foreign Secretary emphasized the need for a diplomatic way forward to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz** and restore regional stability.

## Russia-Ukraine Conflict: Intensified Strikes and Shifting Dynamics

The conflict between **Russia** and **Ukraine** has seen a significant escalation in long-range attacks from both sides. **Russia** launched a wave of missile and drone attacks on **Ukrainian** cities on June 2, killing 22 people and injuring 130 others, with **Dnipro** and **Kyiv** particularly affected. Additional **Russian** strikes on June 9 in **Ukraine's** northeastern **Kharkiv** region killed a pregnant woman and two others, with two more deaths reported in **Donetsk**.

In response, **Ukraine** has continued its intermediate and long-range drone strikes deep into **Russian** territory, targeting energy infrastructure and military assets. Reports indicate **Ukrainian** drones flew over 1,000 kilometers to strike and set ablaze an oil terminal in **St. Petersburg** last week. Other targets have included the **Ilsky** oil refinery, an oil depot in **Ust-Labinsk**, and port infrastructure in **Mariupol**. These strikes have reportedly led to gasoline and basic goods shortages in occupied **Crimea** and are generating discontent within the **Russian** milblogger community. There are also reports that the **Russian** military command is withdrawing forces from the **Kinburn Spit** in **Mykolaiv Oblast** as **Ukraine's** strike campaign renders defenses untenable. In a broader development, **NATO** forces downed a drone over **Latvia** on June 8, after **Russian** electronic warfare systems diverted it into **Latvian** airspace.

## Asia-Pacific: Maritime Disputes and US-China Relations

Tensions in the **South China Sea** persist, with **the Philippines** protesting **China's** deployment of a floating "structure" with personnel on the disputed **Scarborough Shoal**. **Manila** fears this could be part of **Beijing's** efforts to convert the uninhabited atoll into an island base, referencing past instances where small structures evolved into artificial islands. **China**, however, maintains "indisputable sovereignty" over the shoal and adjacent waters, urging **the Philippines** to cease "maritime infringements and provocations". This development comes amidst **China's** annual fishing ban, which encompasses areas within **Vietnam's** exclusive economic zone, and **Vietnam's** ongoing land reclamation efforts in the **Spratly Islands**, adding 216 hectares of artificial land over the past year.

Separately, **US-China** relations have seen recent high-level engagement alongside continued strategic competition. A summit between **US President Donald Trump** and **Chinese President Xi Jinping** in **Beijing** in May aimed to stabilize relations and discussed ways to enhance economic cooperation, including market access and increased **Chinese** investment in **US** industries. However, the **US** has expanded its technological offensive against **China**, blacklisting major tech firms such as **Alibaba**, **Baidu**, and **BYD** as military entities. The **US Trade Representative (USTR)** has also proposed a 12.5 percent tariff on goods from **Chinese** mainland and **Hong Kong** as a result of an investigation into forced labor. Despite these measures, both countries have agreed to establish a board of trade and a board of investment to address mutual concerns. **President Xi Jinping** has introduced a new formulation for **US-China** relations, emphasizing "constructive strategic stability" with "positive stability with cooperation as the mainstay," "stability with moderate competition," "stability with manageable differences," and "an enduring stability with promises of peace".
