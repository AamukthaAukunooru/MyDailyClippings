My Daily Clippings

The geopolitical landscape remains highly dynamic, with escalating tensions in the Middle East, significant leadership changes in **Ukraine**, and continued maritime and trade disputes in the **Asia-Pacific** region. The ongoing **US-Israel** war with **Iran** is drawing considerable financial and military resources, while a newly brokered agreement between **Lebanon** and **Israel** faces immediate challenges. Meanwhile, **Moldova** is making strides in its bid for **European Union** membership.

## Middle East Tensions and Diplomatic Efforts

The **US-Israel** war on **Iran** has incurred significant costs, with **US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth** informing the Senate Appropriations Committee on Tuesday that the conflict has so far cost $37.5 billion. **Hegseth** is seeking an additional $87.6 billion in funding, with $67.1 billion specifically earmarked for the war effort, stating that the Pentagon faces "critical shortfalls" threatening its ability to pay troops and replenish munitions without these funds. This request comes as the **US** has launched fresh strikes on **Iran**, and **President Trump** has warned of retaliation for the deaths of **US** soldiers. **Iran**, in turn, claims to have hit two ships in the **Strait of Hormuz** and targets in **Bahrain** and **Jordan**. The **Strait of Hormuz** has seen significant disruption, with reports of oil tankers catching fire and shipping through the southern lane dropping considerably.

Further complicating the regional situation, **Yemen's** **Houthi** rebels have threatened to attack any ships loading or discharging **Saudi** oil at **Saudi Arabian** ports on the **Red Sea**, declaring a naval blockade against **Saudi Arabia**. This threat has already led to at least two oil tankers carrying **Saudi** crude for **China** and **India** making U-turns in the **Red Sea**, opting for the **Suez Canal** route to avoid the **Houthi**-controlled coast at the mouth of the **Red Sea**. This development could exacerbate global energy market disruptions, particularly as the **Strait of Hormuz** is already affected by the ongoing conflict. **Jordan** is also emerging as a potential frontline state in the widening **US-Iran** conflict.

In a separate but related development, the **Lebanese** army has begun deploying in southern towns designated as "pilot zones" under a **US**-backed framework agreement with **Israel**. This agreement aims to stabilize southern **Lebanon** and facilitate the phased withdrawal of **Israeli** troops, while the **Lebanese** army is tasked with confiscating weapons from **Hezbollah** in these areas. However, tensions have already arisen, with the **Lebanese** army accusing **Israeli** troops of firing near its forces during the deployment in Zawtar al-Gharbiyeh. The **Israeli** military stated that its soldiers fired warning shots into the air after **Lebanese** Armed Forces personnel crossed approximately 150 meters into a security zone and breached barriers, contrary to understandings between the parties. The **Lebanese** army, however, maintained its forces were within the pilot zone.

## European Political and Military Shifts

**Ukraine** has seen a significant shake-up in its military leadership, with **President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** dismissing **General Oleksandr Syrskyi** as commander-in-chief of the armed forces. This decision followed days of mass protests across **Ukraine** demanding **Syrskyi's** removal and the reinstatement of former **Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov**, who was dismissed last week. **Fedorov** was seen by many as a proponent of military modernization, and his removal sparked concerns over the direction of military reform and battlefield strategy. **Zelenskyy** has appointed **Major General Mykhailo Drapatyi** as the new army chief.

Elsewhere in **Europe**, **Moldova's** parliament has elected **Vasile Tofan** as its new prime minister, entrusting the political newcomer with advancing the country's bid to join the **European Union**. **Tofan**, a 44-year-old businessman and investor, is backed by **President Maia Sandu** and her Party of Action and Solidarity. He has outlined a program focused on economic revival and aims to sign an agreement on **Moldova's** accession to the **EU** by the end of 2028, with full membership targeted for 2030. This follows the unexpected resignation of the previous prime minister, **Alexandru Munteanu**, after only eight months in office.

## Asia-Pacific Maritime and Trade Dynamics

Tensions in the **South China Sea** remain high after a **Philippine** Marine was injured in a clash with the **China Coast Guard** at the disputed Second Thomas Shoal. The **Philippine** military accused the **China Coast Guard** of clubbing a Filipino sailor with a baton during the incident, which occurred as **Philippine** personnel approached a grounded vessel serving as a garrison. Both **Manila** and **Beijing** have summoned each other's envoys, with **Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr.** condemning the alleged assault. The **US** has also condemned **China's** "dangerous and aggressive actions" and reaffirmed its commitment to freedom of navigation.

Separately, **China** conducted a live-fire naval exercise near **Japan's** southernmost island of **Okinotorishima**, prompting **Tokyo** to express concern over potential safety threats to nearby vessels. The drills, which also involved a **Russian** frigate, took place within **Japan's** claimed Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ), although **China** disputes **Japan's** claim to an EEZ around **Okinotorishima**, asserting it is a rock and not an island.

In North America, **President Trump** has announced 50% tariffs on most **Canadian** goods, excluding energy products, potash, fish, and critical minerals. This move is in response to what the **Trump** administration describes as **Canada's** unfair discrimination against **American** autos, alcohol, and dairy products. **Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney** has stated that **Canada** is open to negotiations to address these "outstanding issues".
