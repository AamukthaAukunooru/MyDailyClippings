My Daily Clippings

The geopolitical landscape remains highly dynamic, marked by escalating tensions in the Middle East, a critical summit between the **United States** and **China**, and worsening humanitarian crises in several regions. Diplomatic efforts to de-escalate conflicts are encountering significant hurdles, while economic implications, particularly concerning energy markets, are becoming increasingly pronounced. The confluence of these events suggests a period of sustained global uncertainty.

## Middle East Tensions and Energy Security

Tensions in the Middle East have intensified following **President Trump's** rejection of a peace proposal from **Iran**, which he described as “TOTALLY UNACCEPTABLE”. **Iran's** counter-offer reportedly included demands for sanctions relief, compensation for war damage, access to frozen funds, and recognition of its authority over the **Strait of Hormuz**. This diplomatic setback has heightened investor concerns, as the **Strait of Hormuz** is a vital choke point for approximately 20% of global oil shipments, and prolonged disruption could further push energy prices higher and reignite inflation pressures. Oil prices have already moved higher as markets weigh the possibility of continued disruptions to tanker traffic.

The **US** war with **Iran** has reportedly cost around $29 billion, according to the **Pentagon**. In a notable development, **Saudi Arabia** is reported to have launched covert attacks on **Iran** in retaliation for earlier strikes, marking the first known direct military action by **Saudi Arabia** on **Iranian** soil and indicating a more assertive stance against its regional rival. Furthermore, **Kuwait** accused **Iran** of a failed drone attack earlier this month on an island where **China** is assisting in port construction.

Meanwhile, the ceasefire in the broader region remains fragile. **Israel** has continued strikes in southern **Lebanon**, resulting in 13 deaths, including a soldier, a child, and two rescue workers, according to the **Lebanese** health ministry. **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** has stated that the war with **Iran** is "not over," emphasizing that key elements of **Iran's** nuclear program and enriched uranium still need to be addressed. The **United Kingdom** also announced the deployment of its warship **HMS Dragon** to the Middle East, preparing for a potential multinational effort to protect shipping in the **Strait of Hormuz** once conditions allow.

## US-China Relations and Strategic Dialogue

**President Donald Trump** has commenced a two-day summit in **Beijing** with **Chinese President Xi Jinping**, a visit occurring amidst a "tense geopolitical atmosphere". The discussions are anticipated to cover a range of critical issues, including the ongoing war involving **Iran**, trade policies, and the sensitive issue of **Taiwan**. Notably, the **US Treasury** sanctioned nine companies accused of facilitating **IRGC** oil sales to **China** just days before the summit, an action seen as an attempt to pressure **Beijing** regarding the **Hormuz** crisis.

**China** has signaled its intention to make **Taiwan** a central part of the talks, with **Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi** urging the **United States** to "make the right choices" concerning its policies toward the island. However, **US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** stated that **US** policy on **Taiwan** has not changed, emphasizing opposition to any "forced or compelled change in the situation" that would destabilize the world. The summit is expected to see announcements of business and investment agreements, highlighting the deep economic interdependence between the world's two largest economies despite geopolitical frictions. The outcome of these discussions is seen as a defining inflection point for global markets, particularly concerning crude oil, equity indices, and supply chain stability.

## Global Humanitarian Challenges

A new analysis from UN-backed food security experts reveals a severe humanitarian crisis in the **Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC)**, where over 26.5 million people are facing acute hunger. Ongoing conflict in the eastern provinces, particularly involving **Congolese** forces, **Rwanda-backed M23 rebels**, and other armed groups, has driven mass displacement and pushed millions into crisis or emergency levels of hunger. Nearly 3.6 million people are at emergency levels of food security, just one step from famine, with an additional 22.9 million in crisis. Insecurity, funding shortfalls, and the closure of **Goma** airport have severely hampered humanitarian access and aid distribution.

Globally, conflict and violence were the leading drivers of internal displacements in 2025, surpassing disaster-related displacements for the first time on record. A report by the **Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre (IDMC)** indicated a record 32.3 million internal displacements in 2025, a 60% increase compared to 2024. The total number of internally displaced persons has doubled in the last decade, reaching 82.2 million in 2025. **Iran**, with 10 million internal displacements, and the **DRC**, with 9.7 million, together accounted for two-thirds of these conflict-driven displacements.

The **Trump** administration's "Operation Epic Fury" and associated aid cuts are significantly exacerbating global food insecurity. Humanitarian aid funding from the **US** has reportedly decreased from $14 billion in 2024 to $4 billion in 2025. Countries like **Lebanon** are experiencing acute hunger, with 1.24 million people, nearly one in four, facing severe food insecurity, representing a 42% increase over six months. The **United Nations'** $308 million emergency appeal for **Lebanon** is only 41% funded, underscoring critical shortfalls in humanitarian support.
