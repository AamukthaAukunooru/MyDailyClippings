## Middle East: Heightened Tensions and Direct Confrontations

The **Middle East** has witnessed a concerning escalation of hostilities, primarily between the **United States** and **Iran**. Following the downing of a **US** Apache helicopter near the **Strait of Hormuz**, which **US President Donald Trump** attributed to **Iran**, the **US military** launched retaliatory airstrikes on Iranian targets. **US Central Command** described these as "proportional response to unjustified Iranian aggression," targeting Iranian air defense systems, ground control stations, and surveillance radar sites.

**Iran** swiftly responded, with the **Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)** claiming responsibility for drone attacks targeting the **US Fifth Fleet** in **Bahrain** and additional strikes on **US military** facilities in **Kuwait** and **Jordan**. **Jordan** reported intercepting five missiles launched from **Iran** towards the Azraq region, with no material damage or casualties. **Kuwait** also reported engaging hostile aerial targets. **Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Sarakshi** warned that **Tehran** would not leave any attack unanswered and called for foreign military forces to leave the region. **Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmail Baghaei** also accused **US forces** of deliberately bombing civilian water infrastructure in Sirik, Hormozgan province, claiming two water reservoirs were destroyed.

This exchange comes despite a previous temporary ceasefire between the **US** and **Iran** that had been in place since early April, and amid ongoing, yet uncertain, negotiations for a permanent peace deal. **US Vice President JD Vance** indicated that a nuclear agreement with **Iran** could be imminent, potentially finalized before the November midterm elections, though the situation remains fluid. **China** has warned against renewed fighting involving **Iran**, stating that it is in no one's interest and would further worsen regional instability.

Concurrently, the conflict between **Israel** and **Hamas** continues, with **Hamas** reportedly submitting its response to a 15-point roadmap presented by mediators. **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** is navigating domestic political challenges as **Israel** approaches elections, while the **Likud** party has confirmed his intention to seek re-election. In a related development, the **IDF** has reportedly uncovered a significant **Hezbollah** weapons complex in southern **Lebanon**, described by Israeli officials as a "Gaza-style terror infrastructure". This discovery includes advanced surface-to-air missiles, large stockpiles of rockets, anti-tank missiles, and drones. **Israeli** attacks have continued in **Lebanon**, with artillery shelling and airstrikes reported. The **United Kingdom** and allied nations have also imposed sanctions targeting networks enabling settler violence in the **West Bank**, a decision **Israel** has condemned.

## European Developments: Sanctions and Strategic Partnerships

In **Europe**, the **European Union** is finalizing a €6 billion drone support package for **Ukraine**, as **European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen** condemned **Russia's** "longest and most relentless" drone attack against **Ukraine**. The **EU** is also preparing a new package of sanctions against **Russia**, targeting banks, weapons manufacturers, oil traders, refineries, and crypto operators in third countries. This initiative aims to further weaken **Moscow's** ability to finance the ongoing conflict in **Ukraine**.

Separately, the **EU** and the **Republic of Korea** held their 11th Summit in Brussels, reaffirming their strengthening Strategic Partnership across various dimensions, including economic security, trade, and industrial policy. Both sides stressed the importance of a full ceasefire in conflicts and committed to supporting **Ukraine's** recovery and reconstruction.

## African Affairs: Health Crises, Protests, and Economic Agreements

**Africa** is grappling with several significant issues. The **Ebola** outbreak in the **Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC)** and **Uganda** has seen confirmed cases surpass 600, with over 100 deaths. Health authorities warn that insecurity, resource shortages, and travel restrictions are hampering containment efforts.

In **Kenya**, protests have erupted over a **US-backed Ebola** quarantine facility near the town of Nanyuki, leading to clashes that resulted in two deaths. Many residents oppose the project, arguing that **Kenya** should not bear the risks associated with an **Ebola** outbreak. The project has also sparked a legal dispute, with the **Kenyan High Court** twice ordering the government to halt construction and operations at the site.

Meanwhile, in **South Africa**, at least 12 people were killed and nine others injured after gunmen opened fire at an informal settlement near **Johannesburg**. This incident marks the latest mass shooting in the country.

On the economic front, the **European Union** and four states of **Eastern and Southern Africa (ESA4)** – the **Union of the Comoros**, the **Republic of Madagascar**, the **Republic of Mauritius**, and the **Republic of Seychelles** – announced the conclusion of negotiations to deepen their existing **EU-ESA Economic Partnership Agreement (EPA)**. This new **Free Trade Agreement (FTA)** is comprehensive and aims to strengthen trade relations, enhance supply chain resilience, and support sustainable development across the region.
