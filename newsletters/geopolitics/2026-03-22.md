Today's geopolitical landscape features a significant diplomatic thaw between Washington and Beijing, continued realignment in the Sahel, and a landmark G7 agreement on technology governance.

## US-China Trade Talks: Cautious Resumption

Senior trade representatives from the United States and China have convened in Geneva for the first formal trade negotiations since the breakdown of talks nine months ago. The meeting follows back-channel discussions facilitated by Switzerland and represents the first direct engagement at the deputy-minister level since additional tariffs were imposed in the third quarter of 2025.

Both sides have signaled willingness to discuss **partial tariff rollbacks** on non-strategic goods, while maintaining firm positions on semiconductors, rare earth processing, and biotechnology. Analysts interpret the resumption as driven partly by economic pressures on both sides rather than a fundamental shift in strategic competition.

## France's Sahel Exit Complete

France has concluded the withdrawal of its remaining 900 troops from Mali and Niger, ending two decades of military presence in the Sahel. The withdrawal, accelerated following hostile government requests in both countries, leaves a security vacuum that Russian Wagner Group contractors and local armed groups are expected to fill.

The humanitarian situation in the tri-border area between Mali, Niger, and Burkina Faso has deteriorated significantly, with the UN reporting a **47% increase** in civilian displacement over the past six months. The European Union is reviewing its development assistance programs in the region.

## G7 Technology Governance Framework

The G7 foreign ministers have agreed on a joint framework addressing two interlinked issues: AI governance standards for critical infrastructure and a coordinated approach to critical minerals supply chains. The framework establishes mutual recognition of AI safety assessments and commits members to diversifying rare earth processing away from single-nation dependency.

China, which processes approximately 90% of global rare earths, has characterized the framework as **economic coercion**. The framework does not have treaty force but represents the strongest multilateral coordination on technology governance the G7 has achieved to date.
