## U.S.-China Relations and Taiwan

A high-stakes summit between **U.S. President Trump** and **Chinese President Xi Jinping** concluded its first round of talks in **Beijing** on May 14, 2026. The discussions covered a range of topics including trade, the **Middle East**, **Ukraine**, and the **Korean Peninsula**. A central point of contention was the issue of **Taiwan**, with **President Xi** reportedly warning **President Trump** that mishandling the issue could lead to "confrontation or even conflict," pushing the overall **China-U.S.** relationship into a "very dangerous situation". **China's** state-run news agency, Xinhua, emphasized **Taiwan** as the "most important issue" between the two nations.

Despite these warnings, both leaders reportedly struck a positive tone publicly, with **President Trump** calling **Xi** a "great leader" and predicting a "fantastic future together" for their countries. The **White House** readout of the meeting indicated that both leaders agreed the **Strait of Hormuz** must remain open to support the free flow of energy and that **Iran** should never possess nuclear weapons. However, **Chinese** state media's summary of the meeting did not mention **Iran's** nuclear program or the **Strait of Hormuz**, only stating that the **Middle East** was discussed. On the economic front, **Beijing** granted permission for hundreds of **U.S.** slaughterhouses to resume beef shipments to **China** prior to the talks. **President Trump** also extended an invitation for **President Xi** to visit the **White House** in September.

## Middle East Tensions and Conflicts

The **Middle East** remains a focal point of geopolitical tension, with ongoing military actions and diplomatic efforts. **Israeli** airstrikes reportedly targeted seven vehicles in **Lebanon** on May 13, resulting in 12 fatalities, according to the **Lebanese** health ministry. The **Israeli** military stated these strikes hit **Hezbollah** infrastructure in southern **Lebanon**, following earlier presumed **Hezbollah** drone detonations at **UNIFIL** headquarters in Naqoura. **Lebanon** and **Israel** are scheduled to hold another round of direct talks in **Washington**. The **Lebanese** Foreign Minister indicated that **Lebanon's** priority is a lasting truce, while **Tel Aviv** is reportedly demanding the prior disarmament of **Hezbollah**.

In a related development, the **United Arab Emirates** denied claims by **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's** office that he had made a "secret visit" to meet with **UAE** leader **Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed** during the **U.S.-Israeli** war on **Iran**. The **Emirati** Foreign Ministry emphasized that relations between the **UAE** and **Israel** are public under the 2020 **Abraham Accords** and are not based on secrecy. Separately, **Saudi** fighter jets reportedly bombed targets linked to **Iran**-backed militias in **Iraq**, with retaliatory strikes also launched from **Kuwait** into **Iraq**.

**Admiral Brad Cooper**, commander of **U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM)**, informed a Senate committee that the **Iran**-backed "Axis of Resistance," including **Hamas**, **Hezbollah**, and the **Houthis**, has been largely cut off from **Iranian** weapons supply and support. **Admiral Cooper** highlighted a significant reduction in **Iran's** ability to threaten regional partners and **U.S.** interests, stating that **Iran** can no longer attack with the "mass and scale" seen previously, and 90% of its defense industrial base has been destroyed, hindering reconstitution for years. The **Strait of Hormuz**, crucial for global energy supplies, has been effectively blocked since the **U.S.-Israeli** war on **Iran** began in late February.

## Escalation in Ukraine

The conflict in **Ukraine** has seen a renewed escalation. **Russia** launched a significant daytime drone assault on **Ukraine** on May 14, 2026, reportedly killing at least six people and impacting multiple regions. This attack followed the expiration of a three-day **U.S.**-brokered ceasefire. **Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** stated that over 1,560 drones had been launched since the start of Wednesday, marking it as the largest aerial attack since the war began, and warned of potential missile attacks. The strikes targeted residential, railway, port, energy, and other civilian infrastructure across **Ukraine**, including areas near **NATO** borders. In response to these security concerns, **Slovakia** indefinitely closed all its land border crossings with **Ukraine**.
