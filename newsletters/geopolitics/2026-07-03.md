My Daily Clippings

The geopolitical landscape remains dynamic, marked by intensifying conflicts, ongoing diplomatic efforts, and evolving international partnerships. In Eastern Europe, the **Russia-Ukraine War** continues to see significant military actions and strategic shifts, including claims of new weaponry use. The **Middle East** is navigating a period of heightened tensions surrounding the **Strait of Hormuz** and the aftermath of the reported death of a key leader, alongside ongoing indirect negotiations between the **United States** and **Iran**. Simultaneously, North America is grappling with critical trade agreement reviews, while new strategic alliances are being forged in the Indo-Pacific region.

## Eastern Europe and Middle East Strains

The **Russia-Ukraine War** has seen renewed intensity and significant developments over the past 48 hours. **Russia's** Defense Ministry claimed to have intercepted a long-range operational-tactical missile over the past 24 hours, which it described as potentially **Ukraine's** first combat use of such a weapon. **Ukraine** has not publicly confirmed this claim. This comes amid reports of massive **Russian** missile and drone strikes on **Kyiv** and other **Ukrainian** cities on July 2, which resulted in at least 17 deaths and over 90 injuries, with damage to more than 30 locations in **Kyiv**, including residential buildings and a medical facility. In response, **Ukrainian** forces reportedly launched drone strikes on July 1 against a **Russian** defense industry enterprise in the Penza region and an oil refinery in Ufa. Meanwhile, **Russia** is reportedly facing severe fuel shortages across two-thirds of its regions, with **Crimea** declaring a state of emergency and banning fuel sales. To mitigate this, **Russia** has commenced seaborne imports of gasoline from **India**. A study by the Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS) indicates that over two million **Russian** and **Ukrainian** troops have been killed or wounded since February 2022, with **Russia** sustaining a heavier toll.

Tensions in the **Middle East** remain a critical focus, particularly concerning the **Strait of Hormuz** and **US-Iran** relations. Indirect talks between the **United States** and **Iran** are ongoing in **Doha, Qatar**, with both nations indicating a commitment to diplomacy despite underlying mistrust. **Iran**, however, has ruled out direct negotiations with **US** envoys. A fragile, week-long understanding has reportedly been reached to de-escalate the situation in the **Strait of Hormuz** following recent exchanges of fire. Nevertheless, **Iran's** military command has warned that all oil tankers transiting the **Strait of Hormuz** must use **Iran's** approved routes or face a forceful response. This warning comes amidst reports of a power struggle within **Tehran**, with civilian leaders seeking to unfreeze assets and hardline military officials asserting control over the strategic waterway. The **International Transport Workers' Federation** and the **Joint Negotiating Group** continue to designate the **Strait of Hormuz** as a warzone. Compounding regional instability, **Iran's** Supreme Leader was reportedly killed by **US** and **Israeli** strikes, with funeral events scheduled to commence this weekend in **Tehran**. The **US** attack on **Iran** in late February was reportedly intended to prevent nuclear weapons acquisition and reverse strategic consequences in **Iraq** following the collapse of the **Bashar Assad** regime in **Syria** in late 2024.

In a separate but related development impacting international legal frameworks, the **US Department of Justice** formally rejected the jurisdiction of the **International Criminal Court (ICC)** over **Americans** on July 2. Acting Attorney General **Todd Blanche** stated that the **United States** is not a party to the Rome Statute and will not cooperate with any **ICC** investigation, inquiry, summons, or proceeding concerning **US** persons.

## North American Trade and Indo-Pacific Diplomacy

North America is navigating significant trade policy discussions as the **United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA)** approaches its first major review. While the **USMCA** review deadline was July 1, **US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer** indicated that negotiations are likely to extend beyond this date due to the complexity of unresolved disputes. Notably, a commentary by CSIS scholars on July 2 stated that the **United States** has opted not to extend the **USMCA**. This decision introduces structural uncertainty as the three countries must decide whether to extend the deal for another 16 years, renegotiate its terms, or allow it to move toward expiration in 2036. Geopolitical considerations are increasingly influencing site selection for manufacturing, with companies evaluating long-term stability and supply chain resilience in their investment decisions across **North America** and other regions.

In the Indo-Pacific, **Canada** and the **Philippines** have announced a new **Strategic Partnership**, aiming to deepen cooperation across multiple sectors. On July 2, **Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney** welcomed **Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr.** to Vancouver, where they unveiled the partnership covering trade, energy, defense, and tourism. A key objective of this alliance is to conclude negotiations on a **Canada-Philippines free trade agreement** by the end of this year, with projections to triple bilateral trade by 2035. Furthermore, a **Status of Visiting Forces Agreement** between the two nations is set to come into force, which will facilitate closer military training and joint operations, thereby reinforcing collective security and strengthening **Canada's** military presence in the Indo-Pacific.
