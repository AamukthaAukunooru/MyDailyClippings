Energy markets and global supply chains are experiencing considerable strain, prompting nations worldwide to re-evaluate their strategic vulnerabilities and diplomatic approaches.

## Escalation and Diplomacy in the Middle East

The conflict involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** continues to dominate international headlines. Reports indicate coordinated **US** and **Israeli** strikes on **Iranian** strategic targets, including the assassination of **Ali Larijani**, the Secretary of **Iran**'s Supreme National Security Council, on March 17. In response, **Iran** has reportedly retaliated with strikes across at least 12 countries in the region, targeting **Persian Gulf states** and **Israel**, and maintaining a chokehold on the critical **Strait of Hormuz**. Energy infrastructure in **Bahrain**, **Qatar**, and **Saudi Arabia** has sustained significant damage.

Amidst these hostilities, **US President Donald Trump** announced an extension of a pause on striking **Iranian** energy infrastructure until April 6, stating that talks with **Iran** are "going very well." **US** special envoy **Steve Witkoff** reportedly presented a 15-point peace proposal to **Iran**. However, **Iran** has reportedly rejected the proposed terms without assurances from the **US**, including a halt to attacks and recognition of its sovereignty rights in strategic areas. This diplomatic impasse adds complexity to the resolution process, with some analysts suggesting that **Gulf** leaders, despite initial frustrations, now view the neutralization of the **Iranian** regime as essential to their development model and are counseling the **Trump** administration against a premature end to military operations. Notably, **India** has been designated a "friendly nation" by **Iran**, allowing its vessels safe passage through the **Strait of Hormuz**, even as overall commercial shipping traffic through the waterway has reportedly plunged by approximately 95 percent for other nations.

## Global Economic and Energy Fallout

The ongoing **Middle East** conflict has generated significant ripple effects across the global economy, particularly in energy markets and supply chains. Escalating tensions are driving up energy prices and disrupting key shipping lanes, which in turn fuels global inflation. **Brent crude**, the international benchmark for oil pricing, surged to $120 a barrel, reflecting market concerns over sustained disruption. The **European Bank for Reconstruction and Development** has warned that if oil prices remain at $100 per barrel for a prolonged period, global economic growth could be reduced by at least 0.4 percentage points, while inflation could rise by more than 1.5 percentage points.

**Europe** is particularly vulnerable, being heavily dependent on imported oil and gas. European benchmark gas prices have reportedly jumped over 60% since the conflict began, reaching above 50 euros per megawatt hour. This energy crunch is prompting discussions within the **European Union** about potentially scaling back flagship climate policies as energy security becomes a more pressing concern. Similarly, **Asia**, with major economies like **China**, **India**, **Japan**, and **South Korea** relying heavily on **Middle Eastern** crude flows, faces considerable economic pressure. **South Korea**, a critical hub for semiconductor manufacturing, sources roughly 70% of its crude oil from the region and routes over 95% through the **Strait of Hormuz**. In **Bangladesh**, fuel stocks are reportedly tight due to geopolitical tensions, though the government is exploring alternative sourcing and diplomatic efforts to secure supplies. The heightened geopolitical risk has also risen to become a top concern for company boards and insurers regarding their investment portfolios.

## International Responses and Regional Engagements

The broader international community is navigating the implications of the **Middle East** conflict and other regional developments. **US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** has publicly criticized **NATO** for its perceived lack of involvement in efforts to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz**, drawing parallels to the support for **Ukraine**. In the **United Kingdom**, **Prime Minister Keir Starmer**'s handling of the **Iran** war has reportedly aligned with **European** and other **Western** allies, but has also drawn criticism from **US President Trump** and strained relations with **Cyprus** following **Iranian** drone attacks on the island.

In Southeast Asia, **Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto** and **Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim** are scheduled to meet in **Jakarta** on Friday, March 27, to discuss recent geopolitical developments, with a particular focus on the **Middle East** conflict and its implications for regional stability and economic conditions within **ASEAN**. These discussions are expected to explore intensified diplomatic efforts to ease tensions and ensure secure trade routes. Other notable diplomatic engagements include **Russian Prime Minister Mikhail Mishustin**'s working visit to **Kazakhstan** to discuss broader Eurasian collaboration, and a high-profile meeting between **Chinese Vice President Han Zheng** and **South Africa**'s Deputy President **Paul Mashatile**, underscoring ongoing dialogues in various geopolitical spheres.
