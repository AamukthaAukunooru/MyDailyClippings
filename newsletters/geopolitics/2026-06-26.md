## Middle East Volatility and Diplomatic Maneuvers

The **Middle East** is experiencing heightened volatility, largely centered around **Iran** and the critical **Strait of Hormuz**. On Thursday, **Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)** reportedly launched a drone strike on a **Singapore**-flagged cargo ship leaving the strait, damaging its bridge several miles off the coast of **Oman**. **US** officials have attributed the attack to the **IRGC**. This incident follows warnings from the **IRGC** that commercial vessels must use only routes through the **Strait of Hormuz** approved by **Tehran**, deeming a new shipping transit route announced by **Oman** as "unacceptable" and a security risk. The **IRGC** has stated it would take action against vessels that fail to comply with its instructions.

Amidst these tensions, **Iran** and **Oman** are expected to hold talks to define the "future administration" of the **Strait of Hormuz**. However, **US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** has dismissed any suggestion that **Iran** could charge tolls or fees on what he asserted is an international waterway. These developments occur in the context of a recent memorandum of understanding (MoU) reached between the **United States** and **Iran**. While **US President Donald Trump** has highlighted progress in negotiations, **Iran's** chief negotiator, **Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf**, has characterized the MoU as "America's declaration of defeat," emphasizing **Iran's** resistance. **Secretary Rubio** has also reaffirmed the **US** commitment to **Bahrain's** security, a free and open **Strait of Hormuz**, and preventing **Iran** from acquiring a nuclear weapon.

Separately, talks continue regarding the situation between **Israel** and **Lebanon**. **Israel** has indicated it will not withdraw from southern **Lebanon** despite ongoing **US**-mediated discussions, with **Defense Minister Israel Katz** affirming that **Israel's** security requirements must be met. A **US**-backed pilot plan for **Lebanese** army control over parts of southern **Lebanon** is under discussion, though it faces rejection from **Hezbollah**. Reports suggest that both **Jerusalem** and **Beirut** are dissatisfied with the inclusion of a **Lebanon** ceasefire in the **US-Iran** MoU, fearing it could grant **Tehran** continued influence over developments in **Lebanon**.

In response, **Ukrainian** forces have reportedly intensified long-range strikes, with drones knocking out power in **Sevastopol**, **Russian**-occupied **Crimea**, and targeting facilities in **Russia's Nizhny Novgorod, Orenburg**, and **Moscow** regions. **Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** has stated that **Ukraine** will continue preemptive attacks on facilities **Russia** uses to expand its war effort.
