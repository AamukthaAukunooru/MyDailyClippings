The global geopolitical landscape remains highly dynamic, marked by ongoing tensions in the Middle East, strategic maneuvering in the Asia-Pacific, and evolving partnerships in Africa. A fragile ceasefire in the Middle East is facing challenges, while major powers are reiterating their positions on regional security and economic cooperation.

## Middle East Tensions and the Strait of Hormuz

The fragile 10-day ceasefire between **Israel** and **Hezbollah** in **Lebanon**, which began on Friday, April 18, 2026, appears to be largely holding despite sporadic Israeli strikes. Thousands of displaced Lebanese have begun returning to the country's south. **Hezbollah** leader **Naim Qassem** stated on Saturday that the group is "fully cooperating" with the Lebanese government during the ceasefire but would keep its fighters ready. He outlined five conditions for extending the ceasefire, including a permanent cessation of Israeli attacks and complete troop withdrawal, emphasizing that a ceasefire must be observed by both parties. **Israel**'s military, however, has stated it is instructed to use "full force" against threats in Lebanon, even during the truce, and has vowed to demolish homes allegedly used by **Hezbollah**, with demolitions reportedly underway in areas like Bint Jbeil and Mais al-Jabal. The truce is set to expire on Wednesday, April 23, 2026.

Concurrently, a two-week ceasefire involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** is also set to expire on Wednesday. Tensions surrounding the **Strait of Hormuz** remain high. **Iran** briefly reopened the strait on Friday but closed it again the following day after the **US** maintained its blockade on ships traveling to and from Iranian ports. **US President Donald Trump** accused **Iran** of firing on ships, calling it a "total violation of our ceasefire agreement," while **Iran**'s military declared it would retake "strict" control over the strait until **Trump** ended the blockade. The **US Navy** seized an Iranian-flagged vessel, the **Touska**, in the **Gulf of Oman** on Monday, citing sanctions and a history of illegal activity. This incident occurred just ahead of scheduled high-stakes negotiations between the **US** and **Iran** in **Islamabad**, which **Iran** has officially rejected, stating no talks would take place unless the **United States** ended the blockade. Reports suggest **Pakistan** is exerting pressure on **Iran** to ensure the talks proceed. The ongoing instability in the Middle East, particularly concerning **Iran**, is a significant factor affecting global oil prices and energy security.

## Asia-Pacific Strategic Maneuvers

In the **Asia-Pacific** region, **China** has issued a strong warning to the **United States**, **Japan**, and the **Philippines** against "playing with fire" as thousands of troops from the three countries began annual joint military exercises on Monday. Chinese foreign ministry spokesman **Guo Jiakun** stated that blindly binding themselves together in the name of security would "ultimately backfire upon themselves." **China** views such drills as an introduction of external forces and the fomenting of division and confrontation, asserting that the region needs peace and tranquility.

Meanwhile, **Indonesia** is advancing plans for a giant sea wall project to protect the vulnerable northern coast of **Java**. **President Prabowo Subianto** met with officials on Monday to discuss the project's feasibility and technical readiness, with an aim to accelerate the process to meet urgent needs. This initiative is considered a strategic priority to protect industrial areas and over 30 million residents.

## European and African Engagements

The **European Union** has announced the resumption of over €140 million in budget support to **Ethiopia**, citing progress in economic and governance reforms. This decision, outlined by **EU Commissioner for International Partnerships Jozef Síkela** at the **Ethiopia Business Forum** in Addis Ababa, aims to unlock funds for priorities including energy, connectivity, healthcare, and reforms to improve the business climate. A separate Digital Economy Package worth €150 million was also signed to support **Ethiopia**'s digital transformation.

In other developments, the **EU** and the **African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) Secretariat** signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) on April 20, 2026, to strengthen their partnership and accelerate the **AfCFTA**. The MoU, signed by **Commissioner Síkela** and **AfCFTA Secretary-General Wamkele Mene**, aims to boost regional and continental economic integration, promote investment opportunities for African and European businesses, and foster sustainable growth and jobs.

However, the **EU** also faces internal divisions regarding its policy on the Middle East. **EU** members rejected a proposal from **Ireland**, **Spain**, and **Slovenia** to suspend the **EU-Israel Association Agreement** over alleged war crimes in Palestine and Lebanon. These states had called for the **EU** to uphold its "moral and political responsibility" in light of a recent law in the **Knesset** applying the death penalty exclusively to Palestinians convicted of murder, and the dire conditions in **Gaza**. Larger member states, including **Germany** and **Italy**, refused to support the suspension, preventing it from reaching the necessary threshold. **Germany**'s foreign minister, **Johann Wadephul**, stated that while critical of Israeli policy, sanctioning **Israel** would be "inappropriate" and that dialogue is necessary for a two-state solution.

Separately, the **European Union**'s new Entry/Exit System (EES), which went fully live on April 10, 2026, has reportedly caused significant disruptions at airports across the Schengen area, leading to long wait times and missed flights for over 100 passengers. The aviation industry has called for greater flexibility or even suspension of the system, describing the situation as a "systemic failure."
