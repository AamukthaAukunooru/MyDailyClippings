The geopolitical landscape remains dominated by the ongoing conflict in the **Middle East**, now entering its second month, involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran**, alongside various regional proxies. This conflict has led to significant military exchanges, a humanitarian crisis in **Lebanon**, and severe disruptions to global energy markets, particularly impacting the **Strait of Hormuz**. Diplomatic efforts are underway to de-escalate tensions, while the economic repercussions, including soaring oil prices and inflationary pressures, are being felt worldwide.

## Middle East Escalation and Diplomatic Maneuvers

Military operations in the **Middle East** have seen continued strikes by the **US-Israel** coalition against **Iranian** infrastructure and **Iran-backed militia** sites in **Iraq**, as well as against **Hezbollah** targets in **Lebanon**. Reports from March 31 and April 1, 2026, indicate ongoing **US** strikes on **Isfahan**, which hosts major **Iranian** military bases and a nuclear facility, with footage showing explosions. **Iran** has reportedly continued retaliatory attacks, striking densely populated areas in **Israel** and intercepting aerial threats over **Dubai** and other **Gulf states**. The **Emirati Defense Ministry** reported intercepting 36 drones, eight ballistic missiles, and four cruise missiles on March 31. **Iran** has also reportedly threatened **Fujairah Port** and the **UAE's Habshan–Fujairah pipeline**.

The conflict's humanitarian toll is severe, particularly in **Lebanon**, where **Israel** has dispatched ground troops into the southern territories to create a security zone, leading to over 1,000 casualties and nearly one million displaced individuals by late March. The **UN** has warned of a deepening crisis in **Lebanon**, with hundreds of thousands forced from their homes.

Amidst this escalation, diplomatic efforts are intensifying. **China** and **Pakistan** issued a five-point initiative for restoring peace and stability in the **Gulf** and **Middle East** region on April 1, 2026, calling for an immediate cessation of hostilities and the start of peace talks. **Egypt** has also held high-level talks with **Turkey**, **Pakistan**, **Qatar**, and **Bahrain** to coordinate de-escalation efforts, emphasizing diplomacy and dialogue.

Notably, **Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** visited **Saudi Arabia**, the **United Arab Emirates**, and **Qatar** in late March, signing security agreements believed to involve **Ukraine** sharing anti-drone expertise in exchange for financial support and energy supplies. This visit highlights **Ukraine's** growing geopolitical influence and the regional demand for its drone warfare capabilities.

## Economic Fallout and Broader Geopolitical Shifts

The conflict in the **Middle East** has profoundly impacted global energy markets. **Brent crude** prices have surged to around $113 per barrel, driven by attacks on key oil infrastructure and **Iran's** continued stronghold over the **Strait of Hormuz**. The **Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD)** has warned that these disruptions could push global inflation to approximately 4% this year, impacting growth projections for 2026. **Hawaiian Electric** has already stated that customers should expect 20-30% higher bills due to rising oil prices.

The **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical passageway for global energy shipments, faces continued threats, with **Iran's** parliament passing a bill on March 30, 2026, titled the “**Strait of Hormuz Management Plan**,” asserting **Iranian** sovereignty over international waterways in the strait. This has led to global concerns about energy supply chains and overall economic stability.

In other significant developments, the **US Department of State** announced reforms to the **US Foreign Service** on April 1, 2026, under the **Trump administration**, focusing on preparing diplomats for 21st-century challenges and advocating for national interests. These reforms include changes to selection and orientation processes, emphasizing core skills and essential topics like economic strategy.

Concerns over cyberespionage have also resurfaced, with a **Chinese** cyberespionage group, **TA416**, reportedly shifting its targeting back to **Europe** and, for the first time, to the **Middle East** since the start of the **Iran** conflict. This shift is believed to be an effort to gather intelligence on the conflict's trajectory and geopolitical implications.

On the African continent, ongoing conflicts persist. In **Sudan**, the **Rapid Support Forces (RSF)** continued efforts to consolidate control over **North Darfur** in late February and early March, clashing with the **Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF)**. The **Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC)** has seen an increase in aerial strikes by the **Congolese military (FARDC)** against the **March 23 Movement (M23)**, with a drone strike reportedly killing **M23** military spokesperson **Willy Ngoma** on February 24. The **UK** is also set to cut aid to **Africa** by more than half over the next three years, potentially impacting humanitarian efforts in regions like the **DRC**.
