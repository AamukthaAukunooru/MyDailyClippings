My Daily Clippings

The geopolitical landscape has seen significant shifts over the past 48 hours, marked by escalating tensions in the **Middle East**, continued intense fighting and strategic developments in the **Russia-Ukraine War**, and a notable diplomatic push by **India** in the **Indo-Pacific** region. The heightened rhetoric and military actions between the **United States** and **Iran** have brought the critical **Strait of Hormuz** to the forefront, impacting global energy markets. Meanwhile, **Ukraine**'s innovative drone warfare continues to challenge **Russia's** military and economic infrastructure, even as key alliances are solidified.

## Middle East Escalation and the Strait of Hormuz

The conflict between the **United States** and **Iran** has intensified significantly, with both sides engaging in a series of retaliatory military actions. **Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)** launched ballistic missile and drone attacks targeting **US** facilities, including **Prince Hassan Air Base** in **Jordan** and **Al Udeid Air Base** in **Qatar**, following a third wave of **US** airstrikes against **Iran**. This escalation has brought the crucial **Strait of Hormuz** to a critical juncture, with **Iran** declaring its closure and both the **US** and **Iran** asserting control over the waterway. Commercial shipping through the strait, a vital conduit for a fifth of the world's traded crude oil and natural gas, has reportedly halted completely, leading to a surge in oil prices.

**US President Donald Trump** has vowed further strong action against **Iran**, stating on July 13 that the **United States** would "hit them very hard tonight, and we're going to hit them hard tomorrow," and reimposed a blockade on Iranian ports effective July 14. He also issued a direct threat to destroy "Pickaxe Mountain," a site near **Iran's** Natanz uranium enrichment facility, where Western intelligence suspects an undeclared enrichment facility is being built. **US Central Command (CENTCOM)** confirmed that strikes had begun and would continue to impose a heavy cost on **Iranian** forces.

The international community has expressed grave concerns, with **United Nations Secretary-General António Guterres** warning that a return to full-scale hostilities would have "catastrophic consequences." Public opinion within the **US** appears divided, with 62% of Americans disapproving of **Trump's** handling of the military action against **Iran**, while three-quarters of Israelis support the **US** decision to attack, and eight-in-ten Palestinians oppose it.

## Developments in the Russia-Ukraine War

The conflict in **Eastern Europe** continues to evolve, with **Ukraine** making significant tactical gains through its intensified drone campaign. **Ukrainian** forces have reportedly targeted 90 **Russian** vessels in the **Sea of Azov** in less than a week, including 10 tankers and four ferries in a single overnight operation on July 12. This sustained assault has compelled **Russia** to suspend shipping in the **Sea of Azov** and has led to a **Kremlin** diesel export ban, turning economic warfare into a global timeline-setter.

Beyond naval targets, **Ukraine's** long-range drones have also struck key **Russian** energy infrastructure. A major oil refinery in the city of **Syzran** was hit, alongside several electricity substations in occupied **Crimea**. These strikes on refineries and supply routes behind the front line have contributed to fuel shortages and tighter export restrictions within **Russia**, impacting its military's momentum.

In terms of international support, the **United States** and **Ukraine** have reached a co-production agreement for **PAC-3** air defense interceptors, aiming to address critical battlefield shortages. However, the deal is reportedly subject to unspecified financial, political, or intellectual property conditions. Further bolstering **Ukraine's** defense capabilities, **French President Emmanuel Macron** announced on July 13 that **France** would permit **Ukraine** to produce French-made cruise missiles, precision-guided bombs, and air defense interceptor missiles. This follows an order from **Kyiv** for next-generation Franco-Italian air defense systems and **Rafale** fighter jets.

## Indo-Pacific Diplomacy and European Political Strain

In the **Indo-Pacific**, **Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi** has concluded a significant diplomatic tour this month, visiting **Japan, Indonesia, Australia**, and **New Zealand**. During these visits, **Modi** secured important agreements focused on both economic and defense cooperation. This proactive engagement is widely interpreted as a strategic move to establish a powerful economic bloc and a potential military deterrent against **China's** growing influence and access to the oceans. **India's** long-standing border disputes and economic competition with **China**, coupled with concerns over **Chinese** military aid to **Pakistan**, underpin this strategy.

Other regional nations, including **Japan**, **Australia**, **Indonesia**, **New Zealand**, and even **Vietnam**, are also reportedly increasing their military capabilities and fostering collaborative defense ties. This emerging network of alliances and enhanced military power aims to strengthen regional defense against potential **Chinese** aggression, potentially lessening the reliance on the **United States** as the primary force for containment.

Meanwhile, in **Europe**, the **European Council on Foreign Relations (ECFR)** has issued a warning regarding the potential implications of a **Marine Le Pen** presidency in **France**. The **ECFR** suggests that such an outcome could lead to a "structural rupture" within the **European Union**, potentially impacting cohesion on critical issues such as **EU** fiscal rules, **NATO** defense burden-sharing, and joint support initiatives for **Ukraine**. These political dynamics underscore the ongoing challenges to unity and strategic direction within the continent.
