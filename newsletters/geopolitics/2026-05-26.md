Concurrently, the conflict in **Ukraine** continues to fuel instability in **Europe**, with escalatory rhetoric and military maneuvers. ## Middle East Peace Efforts and Regional Tensions

Discussions surrounding a potential peace agreement between the **United States** and **Iran** have been a central focus, with **US President Donald Trump** indicating that a deal to end the war between the two nations has been "largely negotiated, subject to finalization". **US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** also suggested on May 24 that an agreement could be imminent, citing progress over the past 48 hours. The proposed agreement reportedly aims to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical shipping passage that has been largely closed since the **US** and **Israel** launched a war on **Iran** in February. However, conflicting reports have emerged regarding the details, particularly concerning **Iran's** highly enriched uranium stockpile. A senior Iranian source stated that the nuclear issue is not part of the preliminary agreement under discussion and would be addressed separately. **Iran** has also denied agreeing to relinquish control over the **Strait of Hormuz**, with its chief negotiator reportedly stating that the strait will remain "under Iran's management".

Amidst these peace efforts, regional tensions persist. **Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** reportedly told **US President Trump** that **Israel** would maintain its freedom to act against threats in **Lebanon**. On May 25, **Netanyahu** ordered an escalation of the offensive in southern **Lebanon** to "crush" **Hezbollah**, which has been engaged in conflict with **Israel** since March 2.

In a separate development, **President Trump** on May 25 reportedly requested **Saudi Arabia**, **Qatar**, **Pakistan**, **Turkey**, **Egypt**, and **Jordan** to join the **Abraham Accords** en masse to normalize relations with **Israel**. **Pakistan** has rejected this proposal, and public mistrust of **Israel** in these Muslim nations over its military offensive in **Gaza** makes a positive response unlikely from the others.

## Eastern Europe and the Ongoing Russia-Ukraine Conflict

The conflict between **Russia** and **Ukraine** continues to generate significant security concerns in **Europe**. **Russia** conducted drills involving tactical nuclear weapons in **Belarus**, and **Belarusian President Lukashenko** offered to meet with **Ukrainian President Zelenskyy** after **Russia** sent more tactical nuclear weapons to **Belarus**. The **Kremlin** has declared that negotiating with **Ukraine** is "impossible" following a **Ukrainian** strike on a school in **Luhansk**.

On May 25, **Russia** threatened "systematic strikes" on military targets and decision-making centers in **Kyiv**, urging foreigners to leave the city. This warning came a day after **Russia** launched a massive drone and missile attack on **Kyiv**, utilizing the hypersonic **Oreshnik** missile, which resulted in at least two fatalities and damaged residential and public buildings. **Ukraine** reportedly struggled to intercept ballistic missiles due to shortages in air defenses. In a separate incident, **NATO** reportedly shot down a **Ukrainian** drone over **Estonia**.

## China's Diplomatic Engagements and Indo-Pacific Dynamics

**China** has been actively engaged in diplomatic efforts, hosting several leaders and articulating its vision for a multipolar world. **Chinese President Xi Jinping** held talks with **Serbian President Aleksandar Vučić** and **Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif** in **Beijing** on May 25, emphasizing the "ironclad friendship" with both nations. During these meetings, **China** and **Serbia** signed over 20 cooperation documents across various sectors, including trade, technology, and infrastructure, aligning their development strategies under the **Belt and Road Initiative**. **Pakistan** was praised by **Xi** for its "proactive spirit" in mediating peace in the **Middle East**, with both countries agreeing to oppose unilateralism and the Cold War mentality.

Earlier in May, a meeting between **President Xi** and **Russian President Vladimir Putin** concluded with a "Joint Declaration On The Emergence of A Multipolar World and International Relations of A New Type". While both leaders criticized unilateral interference in global trade, the meeting reportedly showed "managed distrust" between the two, with **China** benefiting from **Russia's** dependence on Chinese technology due to Western sanctions.

In the **Indo-Pacific**, the **Quad Foreign Ministers**—from **Australia**, **India**, **Japan**, and the **United States**—are scheduled to meet in **New Delhi** on May 26, 2026. **US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** is visiting **India** to address strained bilateral relations, largely attributed to **US President Donald Trump's** tariff policies. Despite these tensions, both **Rubio** and **Indian External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar** reiterated their commitment to deepening the **US-India strategic partnership**.

Regarding **Taiwan**, **Minister of Foreign Affairs Lin Chia-lung** stated on May 25 that the **US's** position on **Taiwan** remains unchanged following the **Trump-Xi summit**. This comes amidst reports that the **Pentagon** had "paused" a $14 billion arms sale to **Taipei** to address commitments related to the war in **Iran**. **Taiwan** and the **US** continue to share a consistent position on maintaining peace and stability in the **Taiwan Strait**.
