## Middle East: Iran, Gaza, and Regional Stability

Negotiations between the **United States** and **Iran** continue, with **Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Aragchi** meeting **Pakistani Interior Minister Syed Mohsin Naqvi** in **Tehran** to discuss proposals aimed at ending the ongoing conflict. In **Gaza**, an **Israeli** drone strike reportedly killed a 13-year-old boy in northern **Gaza**, and residents have noted a resumption of evacuation orders by **Israeli** forces prior to strikes. The humanitarian situation remains dire, with the **UN's Palestine** agency reporting over 125,000 cases of skin infections linked to rats and parasites in the first five months of the year. Separately, eleven countries, including eight **European** nations, summoned **Israeli** ambassadors and representatives to reprimand them over a video showing **Israeli National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir** overseeing the mistreatment of detained activists from the **Global Sumud Flotilla**. The **United States** also announced sanctions against nine **Hezbollah**-aligned individuals in **Lebanon**, including **Iran's** designated ambassador, for obstructing the peace process and impeding **Hezbollah's** disarmament.

In **Southern Africa**, the **SADC** Ministers of Foreign Affairs held a retreat from May 22-24, 2026, in **Kruger National Park**, **South Africa**. The retreat aimed to assess the impact of evolving global geopolitical developments and collectively chart a path forward for the region, focusing on themes such as infrastructure, trade, energy, migration, and food security. **South African Minister Ronald Lamola** emphasized the need for the region not to be passive observers as the global order reshapes.
