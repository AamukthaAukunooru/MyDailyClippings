## Middle East Tensions Escalate Amidst Dual Military and Diplomatic Tracks

The **Middle East** is experiencing heightened tensions, characterized by continued military exchanges between the **United States** and **Iran**, even as ceasefire negotiations proceed. Reports indicate ongoing **US-Iran** strikes and counter-strikes, raising concerns about escalating risks. Concurrently, **President Donald Trump** has reportedly linked peace terms to the expansion of the **Abraham Accords**, a move that could fundamentally reshape the conflict's resolution architecture and potentially involve **Gulf states** in the negotiating framework.

**Iran** has reportedly threatened to withdraw from peace talks in response to **Israel's** military operations in **Lebanon**. **Israel** has indeed expanded its military campaign in **Lebanon**, moving deeper into the country and capturing strategic positions such as a **Crusader-era Beaufort castle**. **Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** has ordered the **Israel Defense Forces (IDF)** to deepen operations against **Hezbollah**, including potential strikes in **Beirut's Dahiyeh** district, marking a shift in strategy after previously refraining from targeting the Lebanese capital in the current phase of fighting. These actions follow drone attacks by **Hezbollah** that have resulted in Israeli casualties.

**US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** has engaged in diplomatic efforts, speaking with **Lebanese President Joseph Aoun** and **Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu** to propose a plan for "gradual de-escalation" between **Israel** and **Lebanon**. The proposal suggests that **Hezbollah** would cease attacks on **Israel**, in return for **Israel** refraining from escalation in **Beirut**. However, **Lebanese Parliament Speaker Nabih Berri** has insisted that **Israel** must stop firing first.

The critical **Strait of Hormuz** remains a focal point of concern, with its reopening timeline highlighted as an economic chokepoint. **Iran** is reportedly considering conditioning the reopening of the Strait on a halt to Israeli operations in **Lebanon**. Explosions were also reported on **Iran's Qeshm Island**, though an official explanation was not immediately available. Oil prices have seen volatility, surging after initial strikes between the **US** and **Iran** and **Israel's** movements into **Lebanon**, but later falling on improved prospects for a truce.

## Russia Intensifies Aerial Attacks on Ukraine

The conflict in **Ukraine** has seen a significant escalation of **Russian** aerial assaults. On June 2, **Russia** launched a major bombardment across **Ukrainian** cities, including **Kyiv** and **Dnipro**, resulting in over a hundred civilian casualties. This attack involved 656 drones and 73 missiles, notably including more than thirty ballistic missiles, marking one of the largest ballistic bombardments of the entire war.

This latest wave of attacks follows a series of deadly overnight bombing raids since early May, which **Kyiv** views as a new phase in **Kremlin** efforts to target **Ukraine's** civilian population. Analysts suggest these intensified attacks come amid indications that **Russia's** invasion is stalling on the battlefield. **Ukraine** has developed effective air defense strategies against drones and cruise missiles but remains heavily reliant on **US-made Patriot** air defense systems to counter ballistic missiles. There are reports that **Ukraine** is currently facing shortages of the expensive interceptor missiles used by the **Patriot** system.

Despite these challenges, **Ukraine** has reportedly leveraged innovative drone warfare to achieve battlefield momentum against stagnating **Russian** forces. **British** spy chief **Anne Keast-Butler** reported in late May that nearly half a million **Russian** soldiers have been killed during the full-scale invasion. **Ukraine's** effective campaign of mid-range drone strikes targeting **Russian** logistics behind front lines has severely disrupted troop resupply and threatened the "land bridge" connecting **Russia** with the occupied **Crimean peninsula**. In a related development, **France** and its partners intercepted a **Russian** oil tanker in the **Atlantic** over the weekend, citing its circumvention of sanctions. Former **Russian President Dmitry Medvedev** issued a warning that **EU** citizens "will not be able to sleep peacefully" due to their governments' involvement in supplying **Kyiv** with weapons and intelligence.
