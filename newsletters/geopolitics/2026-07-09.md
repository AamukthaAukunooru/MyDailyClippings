My Daily Clippings

The global geopolitical landscape is currently dominated by a significant escalation in the **Middle East**, where a fragile ceasefire between the **United States** and **Iran** appears to have collapsed following renewed hostilities in the **Strait of Hormuz**. This has triggered widespread concerns about regional stability and global economic repercussions, particularly in energy markets. Concurrently, a **NATO** summit in **Ankara** is addressing alliance cohesion and defense spending amid the ongoing conflict in **Ukraine**, which continues to see intense drone warfare. Meanwhile, **China's** recent military activities in the **Pacific** are drawing international attention.

## Middle East on Edge as US-Iran Tensions Flare

Tensions in the **Middle East** have dramatically escalated over the past 48 hours, with the **United States** and **Iran** engaging in reciprocal military strikes. The flare-up began after **Iran** reportedly attacked three commercial vessels in the **Strait of Hormuz** on July 6 and 7. One tanker was struck by an unknown projectile, causing a fire, and two others were hit, at least one by a drone. **Iran's** military stated that vessels failing to use **Iranian-approved transit mechanisms** were targeted. These incidents have threatened to unravel a **Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)** signed less than three weeks prior, which aimed to halt attacks in the crucial waterway.

In response, **U.S. President Donald Trump** ordered a series of retaliatory strikes against **Iran** late on July 7 and into July 8. **U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM)** reported hitting over 80 targets with precision munitions, including **Iranian** air defense systems, coastal radar sites, and 60 small boats belonging to the **Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)**. The strikes were intended to "degrade **Iran's** ability to continue attacking international commerce flowing through the international trade corridor."

**Iran's** central military command warned of a decisive response to this "aggression and terrorist act," subsequently claiming its combat drones struck **U.S.** positions at Sheikh Isa Air Base in **Bahrain** and targets in **Kuwait**. **Kuwait** reported intercepting two ballistic missiles and 13 drones. **Iran's** foreign ministry further stated that **U.S.** actions, including the revocation of a sanctions waiver that had allowed **Iran** to sell oil on the world market, rendered parts of the current ceasefire "ineffective."

The renewed hostilities have sent shockwaves through global markets, with world oil prices soaring more than 5% and international benchmark **Brent North Sea crude** jumping to $78.09 a barrel. Global capital markets experienced severe turbulence, and the **International Monetary Fund (IMF)** has consequently lowered its 2026 world growth forecast to 3.0%, down from 3.1%, citing the lingering risks from the "war in the **Middle East**." The **Strait of Hormuz** remains a critical chokepoint for global energy supplies, and disruptions are likely to impact fuel costs, trade, and economic planning, particularly for **African** nations reassessing their energy security and defense partnerships.

## NATO Summit Addresses Alliance Cohesion and Defense Burdens

The **NATO** summit in **Ankara, Turkey**, is underway with a heavy agenda, as **President Trump** faces pressures related to **Russia's** renewed attacks on **Ukraine** and calls for allies to accelerate defense spending. **NATO Secretary-General Mark Rutte** announced that the alliance would declare tens of billions in new contracts to provide "crucial kit" for deterrence and defense, aiming to demonstrate a "sustainable" alliance where the **U.S.** perceives a "fair deal."

A key development expected at the summit is **President Trump's** anticipated offer to restore **Turkey** to a program that would allow it to purchase **F-35** fighter jets, reversing a ban imposed seven years ago. This move could face opposition in **Congress**. **Trump** is also scheduled to hold bilateral meetings with **Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy** and **Syrian President Ahmed al-Sharaa**. While **NATO** leaders are reportedly keen to avoid confrontation and reaffirm support for **Ukraine**, concerns persist that **Trump's** unpredictable approach could overshadow the summit's substantive discussions.

## Ukraine Conflict Continues Amidst Drone Warfare, China's Pacific Assertions

The conflict in **Ukraine** persists with both sides employing drone technology extensively. **Ukrainian** forces launched drone attacks targeting three **Russian** oil refineries, **Russian** tankers on the **Sea of Azov**, and pipeline pumping stations. In turn, **Russia's Defense Ministry** claimed to have intercepted a significant number of **Ukrainian** drones, reporting at least 452 downed overnight over 16 **Russian** regions, including **Moscow**, as well as over **Russian-occupied Crimea** and the **Azov** and **Black seas**. An analysis suggests that despite the ongoing war, **Russia's** military could reconstitute and emerge more capable and dangerous to **NATO** within five to seven years.

In the **Asia-Pacific** region, **China's** military activities continue to draw international scrutiny. On July 6, **China** conducted a submarine-launched ballistic missile test into the **Pacific Ocean**, its first into international waters. **U.S. State Department** spokesman **Tommy Pigott** described **Beijing's** rapid and opaque nuclear weapons buildup as a concern for the region and the world. This test has heightened regional concerns over **China's** military trajectory. Separately, **Norwegian Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Støre** met with **Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi** in **Oslo** on July 6, discussing the continuation of high-level exchanges and expanded cooperation in areas such as the green economy and artificial intelligence, alongside strengthening coordination on international and regional affairs.
